## Final copy

**Salutation (italic, copper, 18pt):**

> Dee,

(Production: name pulled from report metadata. If no recipient name supplied, default to the property's primary owner per land registry, or to "Friend" — Will's preference.)

**Body of the note (13pt warm-grey serif, three stanzas, italic optional but Will's preference):**

> I prepared this report personally. Every comparable, every measurement, every premium estimate that appears in these pages can be traced to a source.
>
> If anything in here doesn't match the home you live in — a square metre we got wrong, a feature we missed, a comparable that doesn't actually compare — write to me directly. We will fix it and reissue the report.
>
> The conversation begins where the data ends.

**Signature (large, copper italic):**

> Will Simpson
> will@fieldsestate.com.au

**Hairline copper rule.**

**Methodology note (small caps label + 8pt body, lower-third of page, sand on cream):**

> METHODOLOGY NOTE
>
> This report draws on floor-plan extraction, photo and satellite analysis, and street-view scoring of 13 Terrace Court; cross-referenced against 46 Merrimac sales (last 24 months) and 142 active listings across Merrimac, Robina, and Varsity Lakes.
>
> Every claim above can be audited. Source data available on request.

That's the full page.

## Designer notes

- **The note's tone is deliberate.** "I prepared this report personally" is a sentence Will *can* honestly say (Will is personally checking every appraisal at this stage). If at scale that becomes false, replace with "Every claim in this report has been personally checked." Don't promise things that won't survive growth.
- **The phrase "The conversation begins where the data ends"** carries from V2 unchanged. It is the strongest closing line Will has written for this report and should survive into V3.
- **Will's signature is the visual anchor of the page.** Large, copper, italic. Should feel like a physical signature, not a typed name. (Production: if Will has a digitised handwritten signature, use it as a sign-off image instead of a typed name.)
- **Methodology note is small but present.** This is where V2's front-loaded "How This Report Was Built" page lives in V3 — compressed to ~50 words at 8pt. The audit promise is preserved without dominating the document.
- **No additional credits, copyright marks, or registered-trademark notices.** Branding is handled by the footer F mark and "Smarter with data" tagline, identical to every other page.

---

## What this page must achieve

This page is the residual emotion the seller carries with them after closing the document.

By page 13 (the emotional pause) and page 14 (the verdict), the case is made. By page 15, they know what to do next. Page 16 is what they remember about *Will personally*.

Sellers don't hire reports. They hire agents. Page 16 is where the report becomes a person.

The audit promise is the silent guardrail. Most sellers will never write to Will to query a number. The fact that they *could* — and that the offer is in writing on a final, signed page — is what makes the entire 16-page document defensible.
