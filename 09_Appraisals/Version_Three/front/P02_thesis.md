## Final copy

**Headline (large display serif, three-line break, copper accent on the period):**

> Why some homes sell for materially more than others.

**Body (warm-grey serif, 13pt, 1.7 line-height, one logical paragraph per stanza, blank lines between):**

> Most homes are marketed broadly. The highest-performing campaigns are positioned precisely.
>
> Buyers do not pay premiums for houses. They pay premiums for homes that feel difficult to replace.
>
> The purpose of this report is to identify what makes 13 Terrace Court difficult to replace — and to position those advantages in front of the buyer most likely to pay for them.

That's it. Nothing else on the page.

---

## Designer notes

- **No image.** This is the only purely typographic page in the document. The white space *is* the design.
- **Headline composition:** stack on three lines — "Why some homes sell" / "for materially more" / "than others." — set in the cap-serif at 56–64pt. Tight leading (1.05). The period at the end of "others." takes a copper-tinted dot.
- **Hairline copper rule** between headline and body — about 40mm wide, 0.5pt, sits 18mm below the headline.
- **Body sets in three stanzas with blank lines between.** Each stanza is one self-contained idea. The stanzas read like a manifesto, not a paragraph.
- **The phrase "difficult to replace" is the philosophy of the company.** It appears once on this page. It is *not* italicised, *not* bolded, *not* coloured. It carries weight by being the seventh word of stanza two and the third concept stanza three returns to.
- **Vertical rhythm:** headline sits at approximately 35% from page top. Body begins at approximately 55%. Page bottom is empty white space — minimum 25% of page height.
- **Footer:** small F mark, "SMARTER WITH DATA" small caps, page number "— 2 —" right-aligned. Identical footer on every interior page.

---

## What this page must achieve

This page is the single most important page of the report after the cover.

The seller's first emotion was *curiosity* (the cover). This page must produce *recognition* — "yes, that's how I think about my home." The seller must not get to page 03 without having emotionally bought the thesis.

If the seller closes the report after reading page 02, the campaign is already won — they will return.

## What we deliberately removed from V2's inside-front

- "What you are holding represents about forty hours of work on your home." — luxury clients do not buy hours. Apple does not say "this took 40 hours."
- "Floor plans extracted, photos analysed, satellite-tile measured…" — methodology dump. Belongs at the back if anywhere.
- The entire handwritten / italic note format — reads apologetic. Will's voice returns at the closing page where it lands as personal commitment, not preface.
