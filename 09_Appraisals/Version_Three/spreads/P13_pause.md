## Designer notes

- **Image:** a different twilight frame from the cover. Options:
  - The deck-from-above shot
  - The dusk pool with bushland silhouette behind
  - A wider exterior with the bushland edge dominant
- **No header strip, no footer, no page number.** This page is a deliberate break in the document's rhythm. The reader's eye expects another spread layout; instead they get a full-page image and one statement. That dislocation is the emotional point.
- **Text overlay treatment:**
  - Sits in the lower-left third of the page, with at least 60mm margin from each image edge
  - Sand/cream colour (NOT white) — warmer, more luxurious
  - Cap-serif at 24–28pt for the wrap-around statement
  - The four feature words ("dual living" / "bushland boundary" / "cul-de-sac position" / "pool") are stacked one per line, set in slightly smaller cap-serif at 20pt with a copper bullet or em-dash
  - "Yours is one of them." is italic, at 24pt, sitting alone two line-spaces below the feature stack
- **No drop shadow on text.** If the image is too bright in the overlay region, choose a different image. Don't compromise the typography.
- **Note this page restates a four-feature combination, not the five-feature combination from Spread 01.** This is intentional. Spread 01 said "1 of 4 across all five features." This pause page says "1 of 4 across four features." If it makes Will's stomach turn to lose strict numerical consistency, we can either:
  - Restore the five-feature listing (slightly heavier on the page), or
  - Update Spread 01 to also use four features for consistency.
  - **Default decision:** four features here for poetic cadence; verify the count holds at 4 for both versions at production. The headline "yours is one of them" is the load-bearing claim either way.

## What this page must achieve

This page is the emotional climax of the report.

By page 12, the reader has absorbed:
- the rarity finding (S1)
- the buyer (S2)
- the positioning (S3)
- the pre-launch (S4)
- the funnel (S5)

They have processed five spreads of analysis. They need a moment of *pause* before the verdict arrives. This page is that pause.

It works because:
- The visual carries the emotion (a beautiful image of a home like theirs)
- The text restates the single most surprising finding from the entire document
- The closing line — "Yours is one of them." — is identical to the closing line of Spread 01

That repetition is deliberate. The seller heard it once on Spread 01 and processed it analytically. Hearing it again, in this setting, with a beautiful photograph and no surrounding analysis, lands it emotionally.

This is the page they remember. This is the page they show their partner.
