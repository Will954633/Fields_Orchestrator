# Spread 02 — The Buyer

**Pages 05–06.**

After Spread 01 establishes that the home is rare, Spread 02 names the person who will pay for that rarity. One named buyer, with a tangible Saturday-morning hook. Secondary persona as a small ribbon at the bottom.

The emotion this spread must produce: **recognition (of buyer).** "These people already know who is going to buy my home."

This is emotionally devastating to competing agents, who present a generic "active buyer database" and a saved-search list.

### Final copy

**Header strip:** `FOR 13 TERRACE COURT` (small caps), Fields F mark.

**Headline finding (36–40pt cap-serif, two lines):**

> The buyer who pays the premium for your home.

**Hero visual:**
- Lifestyle composition. Twilight pool deck image with bushland edge — same family of imagery as the cover but a different angle. The image carries the persona's *life*, not their face.
- No people in the image. The reader projects themselves in.
- Image ~45% page height, full-width.

**Persona statement (centred-block, 14pt, three short lines):**

> An established owner-occupier,
> aged 50 to 65,
> looking for the last home they will buy.

**Persona detail (12pt, two lines):**

> Financially secure. Moving within the southern Gold Coast. Done with renovation.

**Why-this-home block (13pt, four em-dash lines):**

> Why this home, specifically:
>
> — A cul-de-sac and bushland boundary mean privacy and visual permanence
> — Six bedrooms with dual-living means visiting children and grandchildren never feel like guests
> — A 9/10 finish across every room means no work, ever
> — A north-facing pool deck means Saturday mornings outside, year-round

**Pull quote (italic, with copper hairline rule above):**

> Saturday morning here looks like this: coffee nearby, grandchildren in the pool, no one needing to leave the street, and no future neighbour ever appearing behind you.

This pull quote is the emotional payoff of the spread. It does not need a citation — it is descriptive prose authored by Fields for this specific home.

**Secondary persona ribbon (10pt small caps, single line, then 12pt body, sitting at the bottom of the page above the footer rule):**

> ALSO LIKELY: the multi-generational family.
> 40–55, purchasing for an extended household. The dual-living configuration is built for them.

That's it. No tertiary persona. No catchment math. No willingness-to-pay tables.

## Designer notes

- **The pull quote sits in a forest-green panel.** This is the only forest-green panel in the entire document. V2 used green panels four times across the report; V3 uses one. That makes this single panel the visual anchor of the entire interior.
- **Persona statement formatting** — the three-line break for "An established owner-occupier, / aged 50 to 65, / looking for the last home they will buy" should be honoured in production. The line breaks carry rhythm.
- **No demographic catchment math.** V2 included "10,366 owned-outright dwellings and 16,190 adults aged 50–65 across POA 4226+4227+4220." Cut entirely. The buyer is identified, not quantified, on this page. Quantification, if any, lives on Spread 05 (campaign reach).
- **No "where to find them" footnote.** V2 listed channels per persona. Cut — channels are Spread 05's job.
- **No "willingness to pay $1.85M-$2.05M".** Cut — pricing is Spread 03's job.
- **Tertiary persona (V2's "Out-of-Area Relocator") is fully cut.** Five years from now, this may be the right buyer for some homes. For 13 Terrace Court it isn't. Don't dilute.
- **Image:** prefer a freshly shot or selected lifestyle frame — twilight pool deck, late-afternoon light, bushland visible. If unavailable, use V2's `living.jpg` or `kitchen.jpg` cropped to landscape. Avoid the satellite/aerial here — that lives on Spread 01.

## What this spread must achieve

The seller must finish this page thinking: *"They already know who is going to buy this house."*

Compare the competing emotional outcome the average agent produces: *"They've described what's good about my home in different words than the last agent."*

The difference — between *"this is a good home"* and *"this is the home for this specific person"* — is the entire commercial argument for hiring Fields.
