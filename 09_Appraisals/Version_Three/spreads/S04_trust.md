# Spread 04 — Trust

**Pages 09–10.**

This spread answers the seller's quietest worry: *will the right buyers actually see my home?* Most agents answer with a database size. Fields answers with a different model entirely — buyers arrive at the listing already inside the platform.

The emotion this spread must produce: **trust (in distribution).** "These people already own buyer attention. My home enters a relationship, not a market."

This is one of Fields' most genuinely differentiated capabilities. It is also one of the most poorly explained in V2, which buried it inside dense methodology. V3 makes it obvious: editorial reach + retargeting + 72-hour pre-launch.

### Final copy

**Header strip:** `FOR 13 TERRACE COURT`, Fields F mark.

**Headline finding (36–40pt cap-serif):**

> The 72-hour pre-launch.

**Hero visual — three vertically stacked screenshots / proofs:**

The hero is composed of three proof elements stacked vertically (or arranged in a 2-up + 1 layout if the page allows):

1. **A Fields website screenshot** showing a property article layout, with a "featured slot" indicator highlighted in copper. This is the editorial surface buyers consume before launch.
2. **A market intelligence newsletter or homepage thumbnail** — small, suggesting cadence and reach.
3. **An engagement metric card** — for example, "4,820 unique southern-GC visitors · March 2026 · avg session 4.7 minutes." A simple card; one number; no chart.

These don't have to be identical sizes. Layout flexibility is fine. What matters is they read as *real proof* — actual screenshots and actual numbers, not stylised mockups.

**Source line (small, beneath hero):**

> Source: Fields ecosystem · PostHog telemetry · last 30 days

**Body explanation (13pt, four short stanzas):**

> Three days before your home goes public, it appears in a featured slot on Fields' Property Intelligence editorial section.
>
> The buyers who engage are tagged for retargeting across the entire 28-day campaign.
>
> Most agencies launch on Domain and wait.
>

**Reach footer (10pt small caps line break, then 13pt body, sitting at the bottom above the page footer):**

> In the four weeks before launch, your suburb's bracket reaches roughly 4,800 unique Fields visitors.

That's it. No ten-channel table, no estimated impressions funnel, no audience sizing. (Those live on Spread 05.)

## Designer notes

- **The hero must look like real software.** Not stylised mockups. Use actual Fields website screenshots — the editorial article layout, the market intelligence page, the engagement metrics card. Buyers can tell the difference between a real interface and a marketing illustration; sellers can too.
- **Annotate the screenshots subtly.** A small copper "FEATURED SLOT" tag on the article screenshot, placed where the Property Intelligence editorial slot appears, is enough. No arrows. No extensive labels.
- **The "4,800 unique visitors" footer is a single number on a single line.** It substantiates the "buyers arriving already trusting Fields" claim without becoming an audience-sizing exercise. The reader's response should be "that's a lot," not "where did that number come from?"
- **No reference to specific tools or vendor names** (PostHog, Domain, Realestate.com.au) on the page itself — only in the source line. Sellers don't need to know the toolchain. They need to know the outcome.
- **Forest-green panel is NOT used here.** It's reserved for the Spread 02 pull quote. This page uses cream + copper + the screenshot's own colours.

## What this spread must achieve

The seller must finish this page thinking: *"My home isn't being thrown into a market. It's being introduced to a relationship."*

This is the spread that breaks the seller's mental model of how real-estate marketing works.

The mental model the average seller arrives with:
> Agent lists property → Domain → people see it → they enquire.

The mental model this spread plants:
> Editorial layer → buyer trust → tagged audiences → retargeted campaign → enquiries from already-warm buyers.

That mental shift is the entire commercial advantage of this spread. The seller does not need to understand the ad ops; they need to understand that *Fields is the source of trust, not the supplier of inventory.*
