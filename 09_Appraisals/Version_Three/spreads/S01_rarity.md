# Spread 01 — Rarity

**Pages 03–04. The killer spread. Leads the report.**

This is the single most important spread in the document. If only one spread were retained, this is the one. It is genuinely differentiated, commercially powerful, and emotionally compressive in a way nothing else in the report is.

The emotion this spread must produce: **recognition.** "Nobody else even sees my home this way."

## Designer notes

- **Image annotations are subtle.** The bushland highlight is a 1pt copper stroke at 60% opacity, not a fill. The cul-de-sac circle is a hairline copper ring. We do not want a "data overlay" feeling — this should look like a beautiful aerial photograph that happens to carry a story.
- **No multi-panel composition.** V2's right-page on this spread used a four-panel composition (floor plan + satellite + photo grid + isochrone). Cut to **one image.** The combinatorial filter funnel that V2 ran on its "continued" page is also cut entirely — the headline "1 of 4" makes the filter funnel redundant.
- **The "1 of 4" badge** appears twice: once large as the page headline ("1 of 4."), once small on the image as a copper card. The repetition is deliberate. The reader's eye lands on the headline, then on the image badge, and the data point compounds.
- **The five attributes are listed as five separate lines, not in prose.** The line break is the rhetoric — each em-dash is a beat.
- **No "what this means for your sale" paragraph.** No cross-reference to Spread 02. The closing line "Yours is one of them." carries the entire emotional load.

---

## Why this spread leads

V2 led with "We see your home in more dimensions than any other agency." That spread argued *capability*. V3 leads with **finding**.

The seller does not need to be convinced Fields has analytical capability — that's what the rest of the document does. The seller needs, in their first interior spread, to feel that something has been *seen* about their home that they did not know.

"1 of 4" is that fact. It is verifiable, it is suburb-specific, it is unflattering to no other agent, and it is genuinely surprising even to the homeowner.

This is also the spread that should appear unedited as a teaser image in any pre-meeting communication — it stands alone as a single page.
