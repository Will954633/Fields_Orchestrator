# Property Position Report — Version Three

A complete restructure of the Fields appraisal report, built around a single thesis: **a luxury strategy document, not a data dump.**

V2 tried to prove ten things. V3 proves five things, with one unforgettable idea per spread.

---

## The strategic shift

| | V2 | V3 |
|---|---|---|
| Pages | 36 | 16 |
| Spreads | 10 (× 3 pages each) | 5 (× 2 pages each) |
| Ideas per spread | 5–12 | 1 |
| Charts per spread | 3–4 | 1 maximum, often zero |
| Methodology | Front-loaded (page 4) | Back-loaded footer (page 16) |
| Inside-front note | Apologetic ("forty hours of work…") | **Removed** — replaced by thesis |
| Closing note | Brief | Will's signed personal commitment |
| Personas (Spread 02) | Three with full demographic catchment math | One named primary, one secondary ribbon |
| Pricing argument | Spread 07 with adjustment tables, weighted means, 90% CI | Spread 03 with single positioning band |
| Timing | Dedicated spread (S10) | One line in the verdict |
| Photography spread | Dedicated (S06) | Removed — implied by the photography itself |

The seller's emotional journey:

1. **Curiosity** (thesis) — *"these people see property differently"*
2. **Recognition** (Spread 01 — Rarity) — *"they understand my home better than anyone"*
3. **Recognition** (Spread 02 — Buyer) — *"they understand my buyer"*
4. **Confidence** (Spread 03 — Position) — *"they understand pricing"*
5. **Trust** (Spread 04 — Pre-built audience) — *"they already own buyer attention"*
6. **Trust** (Spread 05 — Campaign reach) — *"this is engineered, not improvised"*
7. **Inevitability** (emotional pause) — *"my home is genuinely rare"*
8. **Decision** (verdict + next steps) — *"we should list with Fields"*

## Spread anatomy (V3)

Every spread is **two pages**, never three. No "continued" pages. Every page has *one* hero idea and as much white space as the design will tolerate.

See [`00_spread_template.md`](./00_spread_template.md) for the full V3 anatomy spec.

**Left page (capability):**
- Spread number (large, copper)
- Single declarative headline
- One paragraph body — three to four sentences maximum
- One supporting line (a quote, a statistic, or a definition)
- Footer rule

**Right page (applied to your home):**
- Section label "FOR 13 TERRACE COURT"
- One hero visual (image, map, chart, or diagram) — full-width
- One headline finding — short, specific, numerical
- A small block of supporting facts (5–7 lines maximum)
- One closing line — emotional, never operational

---

## What we are deliberately NOT doing in V3

- **No "approximately forty hours of work"** — luxury clients don't buy hours.
- **No bulleted "What Fields uniquely does" panels** — the work speaks for itself.
- **No "THE EVIDENCE" callouts on every page** — evidence supports the visual, not the other way around.
- **No three-table data inventories** — replaced by single hero visuals.
- **No "continued" follow-up pages** — every spread closes on its own right page.
- **No methodology dump in the front** — the audit promise lives at the back.
- **No pull quote on every page** — one quote total, on the closing page.
- **No spreads about "we use AI / we have a pipeline"** — proof through output, not process.

## Production

- Same HTML→PDF pipeline as V2 (the brand chrome is already excellent).
- Reuse V2 typography (cap-serif headlines, warm-grey body, copper accents, deep forest green panels, sand background).
- Body type bumped from V2 size for breathing room. Target: 12–13pt body, 1.6–1.8 line-height.
- Hero visuals are the unit of work — each spread's right-page visual is non-negotiable. If the image isn't strong, the page isn't strong.
