## Editorial rules

- **The contents list reads like an argument, not a table of contents.** Each title is a short declarative sentence — "We see your home in more dimensions" — not a generic label like "Section II: Property Analysis."
- **Page numbers are real.** The print edition's page numbers (right column) are computed from the actual page count of front matter + ten spreads + back matter. Confirmed at production time.
- **The "fifteen-minute path" is a specific recommendation.** Spread 01 (data inventory) → Spread 07 (price) → Spread 10 (timing) → Verdict. The reader who skims does not skim randomly; they skim to the spreads we have chosen for them.
- **The audit invitation is repeated here.** The line "if you find a claim that does not stand up, write to Will and we will fix it" appears on three pages of the report (P02 inside-front note, P03 contents, P05 methodology). Repetition makes the offer real.

## What changed in V2

The V1 contents page covered three sections (TRUTH / EVIDENCE / ACTION) over ~24 pages. V2's contents page covers ten spreads over ~50 pages. The structural shift is the headline change in V2 — the seller is no longer being walked through three abstract sections, but ten concrete arguments, each specific enough to read its own spread without needing the full document.
