# P02 — Inside front cover · A Note Before You Read

**Print edition only.** A handwritten note from Will, set in a high-quality script font on heavy uncoated stock.

The note keeps the reader in System 2 mode — slow, deliberate, careful — and sets the tone for the entire document. It is also a vulnerability disclosure: an explicit invitation to find the parts the report got wrong.

---

## Layout

- Single page, no header, no footer.
- Generous margins (top 35mm, side 30mm) — the note sits in the upper-left third of the page.
- Body in the brand script (e.g. *Caveat* or *Architects Daughter*), 14pt, single-column.
- High-resolution signature beneath, hand-signed.

---

## The note (draft, ~110 words)

> Dee,
>
> What you are holding represents about forty hours of work on your home. Floor plans extracted, photos analysed, satellite-tile measured, six comparable sales line-itemised, and forty-six Merrimac transactions used to compute the suburb's adjustment rates. Every claim in this report can be traced back to a source. Every number can be audited.
>
> If anything in here doesn't match the home you live in — a square metre we got wrong, a feature we missed, a comparable that does not actually compare — write to me directly and we will fix it and reissue the report. The conversation begins where the data ends.
>
> Will Simpson
> will@fieldsestate.com.au

---

## Editorial rules for this page

- **First-name salutation.** "Dee," not "Dear Dee" or "To the Vendor."
- **Specific working figures.** "About forty hours" rather than "many hours." "Forty-six Merrimac transactions" rather than "extensive local data." Specificity is the trust signal.
- **The audit invitation is explicit.** "Write to me directly and we will fix it and reissue the report." This is the pratfall effect at work — disclosing fallibility increases credibility on every claim that follows.
- **No commission language. No call-to-action. No marketing close.** The note is not a pitch.
- **Single signature, hand-drawn.** Not a digital reproduction of a signature stamp.
