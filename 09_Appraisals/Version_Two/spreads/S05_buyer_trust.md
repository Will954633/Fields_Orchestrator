### Lede

The conversation an agent has with a buyer at the open home is worth more than everything the seller spent on styling, photography, and marketing combined. The pre-built trust extends that conversation back by weeks. A buyer who has read three Fields articles before walking into your home is starting from a position of credibility — and they negotiate softer against agencies they trust.

### What Fields uniquely does

- **The Property Intelligence editorial layer.** Every active listing on fieldsestate.com.au carries a Fields-written editorial — not the agent's listing copy, an *objective* analysis of the property's price position, value drivers, trade-offs, comparable sales, and verdict. Where agency copy says *"stunning beachside opportunity nestled in a sought-after locale,"* ours says *"the kitchen hasn't been updated since 2008 — a $30K refresh and you're still buying beachside living for $250K under suburb median."* **No other agency in Australia does this.** Fields was the first.

- **The market intelligence pages.** Live, suburb-level data with editorial narrative — published continuously, not buried in quarterly PDFs. Buyers researching their target suburbs find authoritative data here and stay. Average session length on the market metrics pages is 4.7 minutes, which is structurally longer than the Domain grid.

- **The decision feed (`/for-sale-v2`).** Curated property cards with opinionated market judgements — buyers spend 4-7 minutes per session, far longer than they spend on a portal grid. The feed surfaces every active listing in our suburbs, not just Fields' own — because honest market coverage is the trust layer that the franchise model cannot match.

- **The article library.** 53 published articles on Gold Coast economics, agent selection, pricing strategy, market timing, flood data, scarcity research. All liability-safe, all data-backed, all citable. Buyers research before they bid; the research they do trains them to trust our framing.

- **The social reach.** Organic Facebook, paid ad campaigns, Five Property Friday newsletter — all delivering Fields-branded data content to the active buyer pool weekly. The buyer who arrives at your open home has likely seen Fields content in their feed for weeks before the listing existed.

### The evidence

Australian real estate sits at a **−22% net trust score** ([Governance Institute Ethics Index 2025](https://www.realestatebusiness.com.au/industry/30694-public-trust-in-selling-agents-hits-new-low-ethics-index-shows)) — last among ten professions surveyed. The structural correction is not louder marketing; it is honest, queryable analysis published before the listing exists. The Fields decision feed and Property Intelligence editorial layer are both designed to operate from the buyer's side of the relationship, which is precisely what makes them effective from the seller's.

> *"The agency that brings you data your buyers can audit, your partner can challenge, and you can verify against any other appraisal you receive — before you sign anything."*
> **— Will Simpson, [The Science of Getting More for Your Home](https://fieldsestate.com.au/articles/699d7223a47edd0001e077e8).**

### Why this matters for you

Trust is not a soft variable. Trust determines whether a buyer walks into your open home with their guard up or their guard down. Trust determines whether they assume the asking price is a starting point for haggling or a defensible reflection of the home's value. Trust determines how much weight they give to your listing's claims about condition, school catchment, and bushland adjacency. Buyers who trust the source they are reading from negotiate against weaker assumptions — which means the seller carries less of the price-discovery cost.
