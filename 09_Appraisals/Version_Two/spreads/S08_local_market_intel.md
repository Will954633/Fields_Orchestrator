### Lede

Most agencies talk about the market in two tenses: "right now" and "last quarter." We talk about it in three: "right now," "what is changing," and "what is leading the change." The third tense is the one that protects the campaign when the market moves under your feet.

### What Fields uniquely does

- **The Fields Conviction Index (FCI).** A weighted composite of suburb-level signals: indexed price (40%, hedonic, 12-month rolling), sale-to-list ratio (20%), stock-on-market vs five-year baseline (20%), median days-on-market (20% inverse). Z-scored against the suburb's own 5-year history, rebased to FCI = 100 at January 2020. Scale: <80 buyers' advantage, 80-95 cooling, 95-105 balanced, 105-115 firming, 115+ tight/sellers' advantage. Published monthly with a free downloadable CSV. **No other GC agency publishes anything equivalent.**

- **Suburb-specific leading indicators.** Different markets are driven by different forces. *Burleigh Waters*: retail spending is the leading indicator. *Robina*: wage price growth. *Merrimac*: a hybrid of the two, weighted by school-catchment shift. **Interest rates are LAGGING in our markets** — a finding most of the industry assumes is impossible because they have not done the analysis.

- **Nightly pipeline, not quarterly briefing.** The Fields data pipeline runs every night at 20:30 AEST. Stock-on-market drops, sale-to-list ratios shift, days-on-market percentile changes — all picked up the morning after they happen, not at the next quarterly broker briefing.

- **Live market metrics pages.** [fieldsestate.com.au/market-metrics/](https://fieldsestate.com.au/market-metrics/) — only agency in the southern Gold Coast publishing comprehensive, live, suburb-level data with editorial narrative. PRD Burleigh's biannual PDF is the closest local benchmark; we surpass it on cadence (live vs 6-month) and depth (property-level vs suburb-level).

- **Property-level alerts during campaign.** If a directly comparable property lists or sells during your campaign, the daily briefing flags it within 24 hours, with the implication for your price position made explicit. No more waiting for the weekly call to discover your closest comparable just sold $80K below your range.

- **Demand-bracket analysis.** For your recommended listing range, we measure how many active listings sit in the same Domain/realestate.com.au portal bracket. If yours would be the only listing in the $1.95-2.05M Merrimac bracket next weekend, that is a tactical advantage with a measurable impact on enquiry density.

### The evidence

Cotality and Domain hold the underlying transaction data. Neither publishes a named, composite, behaviour-state index aimed at consumers. The Fields Conviction Index is to the GC market what Knight Frank's [PIRI 100](https://www.knightfrank.com/research/article/2024-03-04-piri-100-report) is to global prestige property — a citable, brand-attached, monthly-refreshable instrument for naming the state of the market without hand-waving.

### Why this matters for you

Your campaign will run for three to six weeks. In that time, the market may move. Most agencies either deny the move ("the market is fine") or react to it weeks late ("we should reduce"). The Fields infrastructure surfaces the change the morning it appears in the data, and the daily briefing translates the change into a specific, defensible recommendation: hold the price, adjust the campaign, accept the offer on the table. Without this layer, the seller carries the cost of the agent's information lag.
