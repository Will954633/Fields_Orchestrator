### Lede

There is no number in this report more important than the price recommendation, and there is no recommendation that more deserves to be audited. So we built a valuation engine designed to be audited — by you, by your partner, by any second-opinion you choose to seek.

### What Fields uniquely does

- **Reconciled valuation from 3-8 named comparable sales.** Every comparable named, dated, sourced. Every adjustment line-itemised: land area, internal floor, bedrooms, bathrooms, car spaces, pool, condition, renovation level, view, school catchment, time-to-today.

- **Suburb-specific adjustment rates.** When we adjust a comp by +153 m² of land, we don't apply a national rule of thumb — we compute the suburb's own $/m² rate from its sold cohort. A square metre of land in Merrimac is not worth the same as a square metre in Burleigh Waters; the engine knows the difference.

- **Confidence interval, computed not asserted.** The reconciled value is reported with a 90% confidence interval (`1.645 × weighted_std_dev`), and a confidence level (High / Medium / Low / Very Low) computed from the agreement among the comparables. If the comps disagree, the engine says so honestly rather than averaging the disagreement away.

- **Bracket-aware listing-price recommendation.** [Cardella & Seiler 2016](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/The-Effect-of-Listing-Price-Strategy-on-Real-Estate-Cardella-Seiler/) (n=538): precise prices outperform round; suburb-specific negotiation room is calibrated to local norms (Robina +1-3%, VL +2-4%, BW +3-5%). The recommendation accounts for portal-bracket boundaries — $1,249,000 sits at the top of the $1.0-1.25M bracket; $1,255,000 disappears into the next tier with fewer competitors and fewer searches.

- **Backtested against 1,683 Domain valuations.** The engine's accuracy is measured continuously against the largest external benchmark available, with the median absolute error reported by suburb. Most agency CMAs are not accuracy-tested against anything.

- **Above $2.5M, we publish a comp range and decline a single figure.** Where the comparable cohort is too thin or too heterogeneous to support a single figure with adequate confidence, we publish the range honestly rather than fabricate a precision the data does not justify. Liability and trust both improve as a result.

### The evidence

The Halo Strategy seller research (n=2,351 conversations) found the top question across the Property Valuation theme was: *"Why is there such a wide range in the property valuations provided by different agents?"* (188 mentions, 88 sub-topics). This is the trust-collapse moment that destroys most listing pitches. Two agents arrive, quote $1.45M and $1.65M, the seller has no way to choose. The Fields engine closes that gap by publishing every step of the working.

### Why this matters for you

The price recommendation is the single most consequential number you will receive from any agent. If you cannot audit it, you cannot trust it. If you cannot trust it, you cannot defend it during negotiation. A reconciled valuation built from named, line-item-adjusted comparables is the only document a sceptical second reader cannot dismiss as marketing. It is also the document that gives you something specific to say back to a buyer who pushes the price down on the basis of "the market is soft."
