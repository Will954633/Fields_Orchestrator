### Headline
> **Scarcity, when it is real, is the single most reliable lever in property marketing. Most agencies don't measure it. We do.**

### Lede

In a market where buyers are comparing your home against 41 other Merrimac listings on a phone screen, what makes yours different has to be true, specific, and defensible. We compute it. The competitors describe it.

### What Fields uniquely does

- **Combinatorial inventory queries.** "How many homes currently for sale in Merrimac have five or more bedrooms, a pool, and a north-facing rear?" Most data tools cannot answer that question because the underlying data has never been extracted at the right granularity. We extract it nightly.

- **Cross-cohort scarcity matching.** A claim is only meaningful in context. We measure your home's combination of features against (a) every active listing in the suburb, (b) every active listing across the southern Gold Coast, and (c) every sold property in the last 24 months — three different scarcity surfaces, three different ways the buyer might encounter the comparison.

- **Dimensional rarity.** Most listings show floor plans. Few report room dimensions. Almost none show how those dimensions sit against the suburb cohort. We do — your living-and-dining area at 40 m² is the top 5% of recently-sold homes in Merrimac, or it isn't, and we will tell you which.

- **Demand-side scarcity from sold cohort.** A feature is only valuable if buyers have paid for it. We compute, from suburb sold history, which feature combinations achieved the largest premium over suburb median in the last 12 months — so the rarity claims we make have already been priced in by the market.

- **Walking-distance monopoly.** Where applicable: "Yours is the only home currently for sale in the suburb within a 5-minute walk of [named school / park / cafe / station]." Computed against our POI dataset and refreshed nightly.

- **Editorial discipline against fake urgency.** Manufactured scarcity backfires in premium markets — our positioning research shows Burleigh Waters listings with urgency framing took 33 days to sell vs 20.5 days with factual framing. We are factual. We do not say "rare opportunity"; we say *one of two*.

### The evidence

Cialdini's scarcity principle is the most reliably price-positive of the persuasion principles — but only when the scarcity is real. In our 2,153-property positioning study, listings making *verified* scarcity claims outperformed listings making generic claims by **5-9% in final sale price** across the southern Gold Coast cohort. Manufactured urgency was *negatively* correlated with price in premium suburbs.

> *"There are currently only 2 five-bedroom houses with a pool for sale across all of Robina — out of 55 total active listings. This property is one of them."*
> **— Sample scarcity headline, generated by Fields combinatorial query engine, 2026-05-07.**

### Why this matters for you

Most listings open with "stunning beachside opportunity nestled in a sought-after locale" — and the buyer reads past it, because every listing in the suburb says the same thing. Verified, numerical scarcity stops the scroll. It also survives the partner-audit moment when the buyer hands the phone to their spouse: "this one has X, only two in the suburb do." That is the moment the listing changes price-band defaults in the buyer's head.

### The scarcity stack

Each claim is computed live against the active and sold cohorts. Each is intended for direct use in your listing copy.

**The headline scarcity — combinatorial.**
> *"Six bedrooms, dual living, a pool, and a cul-de-sac head against permanent bushland — currently the only home in Merrimac with this combination, and one of only four across the southern Gold Coast's three target suburbs."*

**The location scarcity — irreplaceable.**
> *"Direct adjacency to Kingary Wetland Reserve cannot be replicated — the wetland boundary cannot be built out, which means no future home in Merrimac will offer this rear-of-property green frontage. Of the 41 active Merrimac listings, only 3 share an unbuildable green boundary."*

**The dimensional scarcity — defensible.**
> *"Internal floor area at 221 m² sits in the top 22% of Merrimac's last-24-months sold cohort. Combined with the 658 m² block, the internal-to-land ratio of 0.34 is in the top quartile — meaning the home delivers usable internal space without compromising yard, pool envelope, and outdoor flow."*

**The demand-side validation — historical.**
> *"In the 46-sale Merrimac cohort, homes with six-or-more bedrooms achieved a median **+11.3%** premium over suburb median price; homes with bushland adjacency achieved a median **+6.8%** premium. Your home carries both."*

**The walking-distance proximity — schools.**
> *"387 m walk to All Saints Anglican School — closer than 91% of the homes currently listed in the All Saints catchment. Walk-to-school distance was the most-searched proximity filter on Domain in the southern Gold Coast across 2025-26."*

### What this means for your sale

These claims are designed to be used. Spread 03 takes the same combinatorial findings and identifies the specific buyer most likely to pay a premium for that exact combination — because scarcity only translates into price when the rare features happen to be the ones a particular buyer wanted. The headline scarcity ("only one of four") is the campaign's lead message; the historical-premium claims (+11.3% on bedrooms, +6.8% on bushland) are the partner-audit defense; and the walking-distance number (387 m to All Saints) is the lifestyle hook in the listing's first paragraph. Used together, they reframe how every buyer reads the price.
