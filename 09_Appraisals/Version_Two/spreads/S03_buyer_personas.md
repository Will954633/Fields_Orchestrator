### Headline
> **Every home has a buyer for whom it is the right home. The price is what that buyer is willing to pay, not what the average buyer is willing to pay.**

### Lede

The premium price for any property is paid by the buyer for whom the home's specific combination of features is exactly what they were looking for. Generic marketing reaches generic buyers, who pay generic prices. We map the home to the person.

### What Fields uniquely does

- **Scarcity-to-archetype matching.** Each rare feature combination identified on Spread 02 is matched to a buyer archetype with established preferences for those features. The match is data-led, drawn from search-behaviour patterns on real estate portals, demographic micro-data, and our own buyer-engagement telemetry.

- **Suburb-specific persona refinement.** A "multi-generational family" buyer in Merrimac is a different person from one in Bondi or Mornington. We refine archetypes against local demographic patterns, school-catchment income data, and observed buyer flow at Fields-hosted open homes across the southern Gold Coast.

- **Price-band willingness mapping.** Personas are not just descriptive — each is anchored to a willingness-to-pay range supported by the historical sold-cohort premium for the features they value. If a feature commands +11% in the cohort, the persona valuing that feature can be expected to bid above suburb median by a similar margin.

- **Three personas, ranked by likelihood and value.** We never present a single buyer because a single buyer is a marketing risk. Three personas — primary, secondary, tertiary — each with their own targeting plan, allow the campaign to maintain reach without diluting message specificity.

- **Anti-fit naming.** A persona is only useful if its boundary is also defined. We name explicitly which buyers this home is *not* for. That allows the campaign to remove search filters and audience overlays that would otherwise waste budget chasing low-conversion traffic.

### The evidence

Specificity bias (Tversky & Kahneman) and the Identifiable Victim Effect (Schelling, Slovic): a named, specific persona generates more inferential commitment from the reader than a generic category. In direct-response marketing, persona-specific creative outperforms broad-audience creative by **2.5-4×** measured by cost-per-qualified-lead across the meta-analysis literature. Applied to property: marketing that addresses the persona who values the scarcity premium converts that persona at a higher rate, which compresses days-on-market and protects the price.

> *"Saturday morning here looks like this: walk 625 metres to Barrio Cafe for coffee, come home to kids in the pool, nobody moves the car."*
> **— Sample lifestyle hook, Fields editorial, 2026-04. The line works because the persona it names is specific enough to recognise themselves in it.**

### Why this matters for you

Without persona mapping, your home's listing copy is written for "everyone who might buy it" — which is no one. The buyer who would pay your home's true premium scrolls past, because nothing in the listing tells them this is the home they have been looking for. Persona-mapped marketing reverses the default — the right buyer feels seen, the wrong buyer self-deselects, and the campaign concentrates around the audience that delivers the price.

### The personas

**01 — The Established Owner-Occupier With Capital** *(Primary — estimated 35% of qualified enquiry flow)*

A 50-65 year-old buyer downsizing internally — moving from a larger Robina or Mermaid Waters home to a single, well-finished, low-maintenance property where they intend to stay for ten-plus years. Household income $200-350K. Children adult or near-adult. Looking for the last home they will buy. The southern Gold Coast catchment holds **10,366 owned-outright dwellings** and **16,190 residents aged 50-65** — the largest single qualified pool any premium $1.95M listing draws from.

*What this home offers them:*
- 9/10 condition — they will not undertake renovation
- Cul-de-sac, bushland boundary, established residential character — visual permanence and quiet
- North-facing rear pool deck — entertaining and grandchildren's visits
- Internal-to-land ratio efficient enough that grounds maintenance is manageable

*Where to find them:* Domain "saved-search" recipients in 4226+4227+4220 over $1.5M. Realestate.com.au "buyer's-club" demographics 50-65, suburb-local. Fields editorial readership in Merrimac, Robina, and Burleigh Waters (PostHog catchment slice).

*Willingness to pay:* Mid-to-top-of-bracket (**$1.85-2.05M**), price-elastic on condition (premium for 9/10 vs 7/10 is meaningful for this archetype). Anchored against the cohort median of $1,287,000 (n=1,069 sold, last 12 months).

**02 — The Multi-Generational Family** *(Secondary — estimated 30%)*

A 40-55 year-old buyer purchasing for an extended household: parents, two-to-three children, and one or two ageing parents living on-site or visiting frequently. Combined household income $250-400K. Currently renting or owning a smaller home in Merrimac, Robina, Mudgeeraba, or Worongary, looking to consolidate three generations under one roof or maintain proximity for in-law care. Smaller raw catchment pool than the Owner-Occupier persona, but the home's six-bedroom dual-living configuration is *specifically* rare-fit for this archetype — feature-match weighting lifts the share above what the raw demographic count would predict.

*What this home offers them:*
- Six-bedrooms-plus-study with dual-living configuration — three-generation occupancy without compromising privacy
- 9/10 condition — no renovation friction during a complex move
- Cul-de-sac head, no through-traffic — safer for children and ageing parents
- 387 m walk to All Saints Anglican — primary catchment for the demographic's school preference

*Where to find them:* Facebook Custom Audiences targeting Merrimac/Robina/Mudgeeraba parents aged 40-55 with private-school-aged children. Domain.com.au saved-search recipients filtered to 5+ bedrooms in 4226. Independent-school parent newsletters (where ad slots are available).

*Willingness to pay:* Top-of-suburb-bracket (**$1.95-2.15M**), supported by the cohort's historical **+40.6% premium for 6+ bedroom homes** and the **+53.1% premium** for the 5+bd-plus-pool combination (n=73 sold, last 12 months).

---

**03 — The High-Income Family Relocating From Out-of-Area** *(Tertiary — estimated 20%)*

A 35-50 year-old professional buyer relocating to the Gold Coast from Sydney, Melbourne, or Brisbane for work or lifestyle reasons. Household income $300-500K. Children primary or early-secondary school age. Time-constrained search — they want to make the decision in 6-8 weeks of weekend visits.

*What this home offers them:*
- Move-in-ready condition removes risk during a relocation
- All Saints Anglican proximity — they have already researched the school as part of the relocation decision
- Bushland adjacency + pool — the "Gold Coast lifestyle" narrative they relocated for, made tangible
- Six bedrooms — flexibility for visiting family during the transition year

*Where to find them:* Sydney-based and Melbourne-based Facebook lookalike audiences modelled on Fields' existing Gold Coast relocation enquiries. LinkedIn ad targeting senior managers within 90 days of LinkedIn-stated move. Domain & realestate.com.au out-of-state browsing telemetry.

*Willingness to pay:* Mid-of-bracket (**$1.80-1.95M**), price-inelastic on condition and school proximity, more elastic on land size. Inflow context: ABS Regional Population reports a net **+9,800 interstate movers into the Gold Coast LGA** in 2022-23, of whom approximately 30% land in the southern GC catchment (POA 4226 + 4227 + 4220).

### What this means for your sale

The three personas above are the targets for the marketing campaign developed across Spreads 04 (where they are), 05 (the trust they already have in Fields), and 06 (the photography and copy that will reach them). The Established Owner-Occupier With Capital is the campaign's lead persona because it represents the largest qualified pool the catchment supplies — 10,366 owned-outright dwellings and 16,190 adults aged 50-65 across POA 4226 + 4227 + 4220. The Multi-Generational Family is the campaign's specificity-payoff persona — fewer in number but higher in feature-match-driven willingness-to-pay. The Relocator widens the geographic net without diluting message. Naming the personas explicitly also gives you a way to read offers as they come in: an offer from a relocating Sydney buyer is not the same as an offer from a local downsizer, and the negotiation strategy on Spread 09 differs accordingly.
