### Headline
> **Neither method is structurally better. The right one depends on the property, the market, and the seller. We compute the recommendation per listing rather than apply a house preference.**

### Lede

Most agencies have a preferred method because their brand depends on it: auction is "their weapon" or private treaty is "the local way." Fields has no brand stake in the choice. The recommendation is computed for each property against the data on the property's behalf, not the agency's.

### What Fields uniquely does

- **Method recommendation per listing.** Each appraisal includes a written recommendation with the data behind it: which method, why this method for this home, what the rejected method would cost.

- **The southern Gold Coast reality, named honestly.** Across our 2,075-sale sold cohort, auctions account for **<2% of target-market transactions**. The market here is private treaty. An agency that pushes auction in Merrimac, Robina, or Burleigh Waters is fighting the market, not working with it.

- **The empirical case for private treaty in our suburbs:**
   - **Engagement on realestate.com.au** is structurally higher for priced private-treaty listings than for "Auction" / "Contact Agent" listings — buyers filter by price band, and unpriced listings drop out of the bracket.
   - **Lower seller pressure.** No 30-day countdown. No public theatre. Negotiation happens privately, with the seller's name and home not splashed across an auction broadcast.
   - **No QLD price-guide weirdness.** QLD bans advertised price guides on auctions, which forces verbal-range discussions that incentivise underquoting. Private treaty exposes the price honestly.
   - **Time-control.** The seller can extend, withdraw, accept early — choices auction strips away once the date is set.

- **Where auction is the right call:**
   - Properties where competitive bidding is structurally likely (one-of-a-kind, limited comps, very high demand)
   - Sellers with strong tolerance for public process and a hard timeline
   - Markets where clearance rates are running 75%+ for the relevant archetype
   - Properties where price discovery is genuinely uncertain and the buyer pool is concentrated enough to surface in a single moment

- **Open-home flexibility around the seller, not the agent.** Inspection times scheduled around your week — weekday twilights, Sunday mornings, by-appointment private inspections — engineered around your work, your kids, your life. Not "Saturday 11am because that is when our agent is free."

### The evidence

- Frino, Peat & Wright 2012 (n=1.2M Australian sales): the auction premium is method-by-market and disappears entirely in markets with thin bidding density. Auction in a market with <2% method share is structurally a poor fit.
- REA Insights 2014: priced listings receive significantly higher engagement than unpriced "Contact Agent" / "Auction" listings on realestate.com.au.
- Halo Strategy seller research: trust and control are the two themes most damaged by an inappropriate method choice. *"Will my agent push auction because it's good for them, not for me?"* is the implicit question the recommendation must answer.

### Why this matters for you

The method decision shapes everything that follows: the marketing, the buyer behaviour, the timeline, the negotiation dynamics, the cost, the seller's stress level. It is one of two or three decisions in a campaign where the agent's incentives can run directly counter to the seller's. The Fields recommendation is computed on the data, not the brand — which means the seller never has to second-guess whether the method serves them.

### Why auction was rejected

Three specific reasons documented for the seller's audit:

**1. Method-share data.** Across the southern Gold Coast 2,075-sale sold cohort, auctions account for <2% of transactions in the $1.5-2.5M bracket. The market here is structurally private treaty. An auction in Merrimac fights the market default.

**2. Buyer-pool concentration.** The three personas on Spread 03 are not a concentrated, single-Saturday-bidding-room buyer pool — they are a distributed pool of 380 qualified buyers active across the next 30 days, sourced through different channels and reaching the listing on different days. A 28-day private-treaty window allows each persona to engage on their own timeline; an auction compresses that into a single event for which only a fraction of the pool will be ready.

**3. Seller risk profile.** Auction transfers the price-discovery cost to the seller via the on-the-day result. A rejected auction in this market means the listing reverts to private treaty with the public knowledge that the home did not sell — a starting position weaker than launching as private treaty in the first place. The downside risk of auction is asymmetrically larger than the upside potential for a property of this archetype in this market.
