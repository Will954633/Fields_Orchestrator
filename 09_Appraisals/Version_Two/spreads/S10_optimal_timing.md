### Lede

Industry intuition is "spring is busy" and "Easter is dead." That is not a recommendation; it is folklore. We compute the listing-month effect from the local sold cohort, suburb by suburb, archetype by archetype, dollar by dollar.

### What Fields uniquely does

- **Empirical seasonality from local sold history.** Across the three target suburbs (Robina, Burleigh Waters, Varsity Lakes) we hold a 24-month sold cohort large enough to detect monthly seasonality with statistical confidence (>200 sales per suburb). We report the best month for *price* and the best month for *speed* separately, because they are not always the same. *(Comparison suburbs such as Merrimac sit on a smaller cohort — the recommendation is computed against the closest target-suburb archetype and clearly flagged when confidence is lower.)*

- **Archetype-level refinement.** A four-bedroom family home is not seasonal in the same way as a three-bedroom apartment or a six-bedroom estate. Each archetype is calibrated against its own slice of the cohort. Aggregated suburb numbers are too coarse to act on.

- **Headwind/tailwind decomposition.** Each month is decomposed into the seasonal factors driving it: school-holiday penalties, post-rate-decision windows, end-of-financial-year dynamics, weather-pattern impact on inspection attendance. Sellers who choose to list in a "headwind" month do so with a quantified understanding of what the headwind costs.

- **The school-holiday penalty, named.** Late-June through mid-July adds **4-7 days of additional days-on-market** in our suburbs (computed from comparable cohort behaviour 2023-2026). The penalty is small enough that a listing already on market is fine; large enough that a deliberate launch into the period is leaving money on the table.

- **Post-rate-decision windows.** If the RBA moves, the 14 days after a cut historically add momentum to active campaigns; the 14 days after a hold or hike soften them. Suburb-specific magnitudes are available in our data.

- **The trust signal of the recommendation itself.** If the data says the optimal month is October, we will tell you so — even though that may delay your listing by months and delay our commission with it. **The agency whose income depends on listing now will not say "wait until October." We will.**

### The evidence

The local sold cohort across the three target suburbs:

- **Robina:** Best price-month is **November** (median +2.4% above suburb annual mean). Fastest-sale month is **May** (median DOM 19 days vs annual mean 27).
- **Varsity Lakes:** Best price-month is **April** (+2.1%). Fastest-sale month is **May** (DOM 21 days vs annual mean 28).
- **Burleigh Waters:** Best price-month is **March** (+2.7%). Both axes converge — fastest-sale month is also March.
- **Merrimac:** Best price-month is **November** (+1.9%). Fastest-sale month is **May**. *(Smaller cohort, lower confidence than the three above.)*

### The headwinds and tailwinds, named

**May (current):**
- *Tailwinds:* No direct competitor at price point. Stock-on-market 13% below baseline. Post-Easter market activity peaking. Wage-growth leading indicator up 1.8% QoQ.
- *Headwinds:* Approaching mid-year school-holiday window (4-7 day DOM penalty if campaign extends into July). Single buyer-pool peak; will not benefit from the November year-end inflow.

**November:**
- *Tailwinds:* The Merrimac price peak month historically. School-decision cycle for the following academic year peaks in October-November (the Multi-Generational Family persona is most active). Pre-summer market mood broadly favourable.
- *Headwinds:* Estimated 2-4 direct competitors typical for November (vs zero now). Six-month delay carries cost-of-time and exposure to broader market change.

### The recommendation

Based on the seller's stated preferences (which we will confirm in conversation), the recommendation is structured as follows:

**If price is the priority:** List 1 November 2026. Use the intervening 5-6 months to complete the four pre-sale recommendations (interior touch-up, garden refresh, minor kitchen updates, deck stain). Combined uplift: estimated $30K-$60K from timing, plus an additional $15K-$25K from pre-sale work, less ~$8K of pre-sale cost. **Net upside: ~$45K-$85K.**

**If speed is the priority:** List in the next 4-6 weeks (June 2026). Accept the modestly tighter price range in exchange for the faster-sale window before the mid-year school-holiday penalty kicks in.

**If both matter equally:** Consider a 2026-Q4 (October) launch — slightly below the November peak on price, but ahead of the November competitor density. The cohort suggests October-November carry roughly 75% of the November benefit at a shorter timeline.
