# Back · Inside Back Cover · A Final Note

**Print edition only.** A short handwritten close from Will. The last thing the reader sees.

The note names the property. Wishes the seller well. Does not ask for the listing.

---

## Layout

- Single page, no header, no footer.
- Generous margins.
- Body in the brand script (matches P02 inside-front note), 14pt.
- High-resolution signature beneath, hand-signed.
- The Fields F-mark and "Smarter with data" tagline at the bottom of the page in 7pt — quiet.

---

## The note (draft, ~50 words)

> Dee,
>
> Whatever you decide for 13 Terrace Court — and whether or not Fields plays a part in it — I hope this report is the most thorough piece of analysis your home has received from anyone.
>
> Will

---

## Editorial rules

- **Maximum 60 words.** Anything longer reads as a marketing close, which the page is not.
- **First-name salutation. Hand-signed signature.** Same conventions as the inside-front note (P02), creating a paired bookend.
- **The address is named.** "13 Terrace Court" makes the note specific. A generic "I hope this report serves you well" does not earn the page.
- **No call-to-action. No "we look forward to hearing from you." No commission discussion.** The page exists to mark the end of the document, not to extend the pitch.
- **The peak-end principle applies to the document as it does to the open home.** The last thing the reader sees defines what they remember of the entire experience. A handwritten note is the most peak-end-appropriate object the document can end with.

---

## Back cover (the very last page)

A separate page after the inside-back note:
- **Full-bleed black.**
- **Embossed Fields logomark in copper foil.** Nothing else.
- Heavy uncoated stock, lay-flat binding.
- The book opens beautifully on the dining table; the back cover is the silent close that bookends the front cover's twilight hero.
