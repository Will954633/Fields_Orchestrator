This document defines the strategic intent and operating principles for the Fields Appraisal Report. It is the parent of every other document in this folder. If a design decision conflicts with this strategy, this strategy wins.

## 1. The single sentence

**The Fields Appraisal Report is the most thorough, transparent, beautifully-built case any homeowner has ever received about their own home — designed so that, after reading it, choosing any other agent feels irrational.**

Everything below is in service of that sentence.

---

## 2. The job-to-be-done

A homeowner does not order an appraisal because they want a number. They order one because they have a question they cannot answer alone:

> "If I sell, will I be in safe hands, and will I get what my home is actually worth?"

That question hides three sub-questions, each terrifying in its own way:

1. **Truth** — *What is my home actually worth, and is the person telling me being honest?*
2. **Competence** — *Does this person know more than I do, and can they prove it?*
3. **Care** — *Will they treat my home, my move, and my money like it matters?*

Every page of the report must answer one of these three sub-questions. If a page does not, it gets cut.

This is the single biggest divergence from competitor reports. A typical Australian agent appraisal answers only the first sub-question, partially, and uses the rest of the document to advertise the agency. We invert the ratio.

## 3. The reader

We have three readers, and the report must serve all of them simultaneously.

**Reader A — The Skim-Reader (60 seconds).** Walks in from the kitchen, flips through, glances at headlines, looks at the price range, looks at the photos, decides whether to keep reading. We need to win this reader at first contact. They are System 1: visual, emotional, fast.

**Reader B — The Compare-Shopper (10 minutes).** Has two or three other appraisals on the table. Reading side-by-side. Looking for differences. Will count comparable sales, scrutinise adjustments, judge depth of analysis. They are System 1 + System 2 in tandem.

**Reader C — The Deep-Reader (60+ minutes, often a partner).** Reads cover-to-cover, often a second time. Often the more analytical of a couple. Will Google our citations. Will challenge any number that doesn't add up. They are System 2: forensic, sceptical, rewarding rigour.

Each page must be designed in three layers:

- **A 5-second layer** — what the reader takes away from a single glance (headline + chart + colour signal)
- **A 60-second layer** — what they take away from skimming (sub-headlines, callouts, key numbers, captions)
- **A full-depth layer** — the prose, citations, methodology notes, footnotes

Most agent reports operate on one layer. Magazines like *The Economist*, *Monocle* and *The Financial Times* operate on three. We operate on three.

## 4. Strategic principles (non-negotiable)

### 4.1 Transparency as a weapon
Every number in the report is built from a visible chain of evidence. Every comparable, every adjustment, every dollar of impact, every confidence band — shown, sourced, dated. **No black boxes.** This is the single biggest competitive moat available to us, because incumbents structurally cannot copy it without exposing how thin their numbers are.

### 4.2 Honesty as the trust mechanism
The report names the property's weaknesses before its strengths. Every trade-off is acknowledged — and *then* reframed as value. (See `04_psychology_principles.md` §Inoculation Effect.) A seller reading our report should think: *"They told me the truth I didn't want to hear, and they had an answer for it."* That is the moment trust transfers.

### 4.3 Specificity over fluency
"Stunning" is fluency. "658 m² lot, 107 m² less than the nearest comparable, $40,000–$63,000 less land value, but the outdoor package compensates by $95,000–$145,000" is specificity. We use specificity. The forbidden words list (`stunning`, `nestled`, `boasting`, `rare opportunity`, `robust market`) is enforced at editorial review.

### 4.4 Local mastery as the credibility floor
We name streets. We name schools. We name cafes. We name reserve boundaries. The reader must close the report believing: *"They know my street better than I do."* This is what makes the Harvard-negotiation badge believable instead of corporate.

### 4.5 Never tell the seller what to do
Liability and trust both demand this. We say "we would" not "you should". We say "the data suggests" not "this means". We present evidence; the seller draws conclusions. (See `04_psychology_principles.md` §Reactance.)

### 4.6 The report is the product
Many agencies treat the appraisal as a *door-opener* — a perfunctory document that exists to win a meeting. We invert that: the meeting is what *the report earns*. A report so good the seller wants to meet the person who made it. This shifts the entire production calculus.

## 5. The competitive moat (why this is defensible)

Most premium agencies in Australia produce what we call **listing-presentation pitch decks**: 30–60 page documents heavy on photography of past listings, agency awards, agent biographies, and a single-page CMA at the back. They are built by the agent's marketing department, designed to sell *the agent*, lightly customised per property (often only the cover and the address).

Fields' moat sits on three pillars they structurally cannot copy:

**Pillar 1 — Data infrastructure.** We have built a full property-data pipeline:
- 2,153+ analysed sold properties across the Gold Coast
- Suburb-specific adjustment rates derived from 254+ sales with floor-area data
- 1,689 Domain estimates tested against actual sale prices
- 1,475 Gold Coast sales analysed by agent volume and outcome
- Daily-refreshed market metrics (median, DOM, listings, absorption)
- Per-property satellite analysis, photo analysis, room scoring, flood modelling
- 60+ studies and 14 academic papers in our positioning library

A franchise agent has none of this. They have RP Data and a memory.

**Pillar 2 — Editorial method.** A seven-stage AI pipeline (price agent → property agent → market agent → editor → reflection → fact-check → re-draft) producing per-property prose that cites comparable addresses, dollar adjustments, named amenities, and tested research. This produces 11 pages of fully-customised *thinking* that no human-written competitor report can match for cost or speed.

**Pillar 3 — Visual + interactive layer.** A hybrid digital-and-print product (see `07_production_plan.md`) with:
- Print-grade InDesign typography and information design
- A personalised microsite that extends the print
- Embedded video (Will reading the analysis)
- Live, link-back updates as the market shifts after the appraisal is sent
- View-tracking telemetry so we know exactly what the seller engaged with

A competitor would need to staff a design studio, a data team, a content team, and a video team — all of which Fields already has, because Will built them as a single operator using AI.

**The moat is not any single pillar. It is that we have all three.**

## 6. Format strategy: the three editions

The report is delivered in three coordinated editions, each tuned to a moment in the seller journey.

### Edition 1 — The Digital First-Drop
Sent within 48 hours of address submission. PDF + microsite link. Designed for the kitchen-table moment when the seller first opens it. Focus: evidence overload, building credibility before the seller has had time to compare us against anyone else. **Goal: be the benchmark by which they judge every other agent.**

### Edition 2 — The Print Edition
Hand-delivered or couriered after the first conversation. Lay-flat A4 magazine, perfect-bound, 32–40 pages. Premium uncoated paper for prose, coated for photography. Includes a tipped-in QR card linking to Will's video walkthrough of the report. **Goal: be the document that sits on the dining table for two weeks, that gets shown to family members, that becomes part of the seller's selling story.**

### Edition 3 — The Living Microsite
A personalised URL (e.g. `fieldsestate.com.au/appraisals/13-terrace-court`) that updates automatically as new data arrives — new comparable sales, market shifts, demand-bracket changes. Seller can return to it any time. Forms the basis of the post-listing campaign dashboard. **Goal: extend our presence in the seller's life from 30 minutes (one-time PDF read) to 30+ days of recurring touchpoints.**

The three editions share a single content pipeline. Print is rendered from the same data layer as digital. (See `07_production_plan.md`.)

## 7. What we will deliberately *not* do

Defining the negative space is as important as defining the positive.

- **No agent biography page.** A homeowner does not need to read Will's resume; the rigour of the analysis IS the resume.
- **No "Why Choose Fields" page padded with awards.** The whole report is why. A two-line panel on the back is plenty.
- **No testimonial wallpaper.** One thoughtfully-chosen, full-bleed quote, sparingly. Pre-revenue we will use the analyst-to-seller dialogue from the report itself as testimonial.
- **No stock photography.** Every photograph is local, attributable, and named. Stock photography signals "we don't actually know your area."
- **No predictions.** "The Robina market will…" is forbidden. "Our analysis of 13,585 GC sales over six years shows a historical pattern of…" is allowed.
- **No advice.** "We would" not "You should". Always.
- **No round-number valuations in the body.** $1,765,000–$1,825,000, not $1.75M–$1.85M. (Cardella & Seiler 2016.)
- **No price guarantees.** Implied or otherwise.

## 8. Success metrics

The report is working when:

- **Appraisal-to-listing conversion** ≥ 60% (industry benchmark for high-trust agents is ~30–40%; AREC presentations cite Tom Panos's "1 in 3" rule).
- **Median time-on-document** ≥ 18 minutes for the digital edition (Dee spent 51 minutes on page 1 alone — that is the ceiling).
- **Multi-session opens** — at least 3 separate sessions per recipient on average. Indicates the seller returned to it (Dee opened 6 times).
- **Page-11 engagement** ("Why Fields + Next Steps") ≥ 70% reach. If sellers stop reading at the comparables, the back of the report has failed.
- **Print-edition keep-rate** — 12 weeks after listing, ≥ 50% of sellers can show us where the report sits in their home (we ask informally during settlement).
- **Verbatim quote test** — when a Fields seller is asked by a friend why they picked us, ≥ 50% of the answer should reference *something specific from the report*, not generic feelings about Will.

These are aspirational while we are pre-revenue. We will track them from the first 10 reports onwards via the existing tracking-server telemetry.

## 9. The deeper bet

The conventional wisdom in real estate is that listings are won in meetings — by chemistry, charm, body language, and a strong close. We are betting that the meeting is downstream of the document, and that an unbeatable document collapses the meeting into a formality. If that bet is correct, we can run a real estate business with one operator and an AI workshop, scale by 10x without losing intimacy, and charge a premium fee that competitors structurally cannot match.

Every line item in this strategy folder is a wager on that bet.
