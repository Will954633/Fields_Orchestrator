# Report Blueprint — Page by Page

This document specifies every page of the Fields Appraisal Report across its three editions (Digital Quick-Read, Print Master, Living Microsite). It is the single source of truth for what goes where, why, and how it links to data.

Read after `00_strategy.md` and `01_psychology_principles.md`. Each page entry lists: (a) Job-to-be-done, (b) Reader takeaway in 5 / 60 / 600 seconds, (c) Data inputs, (d) Psychology principles applied, (e) Production notes.

---

## The two structural choices that everything follows from

**Choice 1 — Three readers, three layers, every page.** Headline + chart + 5-second visual signal (skim layer) → sub-headlines + callouts + key numbers (60-second layer) → prose, citations, methodology (deep layer). Enforced on every spread.

**Choice 2 — Three sections, three tabs.** TRUTH (what the home is worth and why), EVIDENCE (the data layer), ACTION (what to do next). Tabbed dividers let any reader flip directly to the part they care about. Print uses physical dividers; digital uses sticky-tab navigation.

---

| Edition | Pages | Format | Sent when |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Digital Quick-Read** | 12 | A4 PDF, single-column, screen-first | Within 48h of address submission |

Same content pipeline drives all three. Print expands the digital with deep-dive spreads + photography. Microsite extends the print with motion, video, and live data.

# SECTION I — TRUTH (digital pp. 2–5; print pp. 5–14)

The analytical core. What is this home worth, and why.

## P5 · Section divider — TRUTH
**JTBD:** Restful pause, anchoring transition.

- Full-bleed copper colour field. Single-line definition: *Truth — what the data shows, before we say anything about what to do with it.*
- A single inset photograph of the property's most distinctive feature.

## P6 · The Fields Take (digital P2; print P6–7 spread)
**JTBD:** The verdict that earns 18 minutes of attention.

**5-second:** A single declarative headline (e.g. "Your property sits well above the Merrimac median, supported by three recent comparable sales") + the verdict box.
**60-second:** Specs pills + strengths panel + trade-off panel.
**Deep:** The verdict prose.

- **Headline rule:** must include a precise numerical anchor (e.g. "three", "650m²", "$1.7M+").
- **Verdict box:** copper-on-grass colour-block, 90–110 words. Must lead with "Based on N adjusted comparable sales ranging from $X to $Y, we estimate a most likely selling range of $A–$B…" Then drivers, then conclusion.
- **Strengths:** 3–4 bullets, each with a dollar quantification. *No qualitative bullets allowed.*
- **Key Trade-Off:** named, dollar-quantified, with the reframe in bold below. (Inoculation in action.)

**Print bonus:** opposite page is a full-bleed twilight exterior. The contrast (analysis vs. emotion) creates the magazine feel.

## P7 · The Comparable Evidence (digital P3; print P8–9 spread)
**JTBD:** Anchoring through evidence, not assertion.

**5-second:** Three comp cards laid out as a forensic table.
**60-second:** Each card shows the comp's specs + line-item adjustments + adjusted-to-yours value, with the time adjustment itemised.
**Deep:** Footnotes on adjustment rates derived from N suburb sales; link to the methodology page.

- **Adjustment line items:** land area, internal floor, bedrooms, bathrooms, car spaces, pool, condition, renovation level, view, school catchment delta, time-to-today. Each with a precise sign and dollar value.
- **The reveal:** at the bottom, the *Comparable-Adjusted Valuation* banner: most-likely range + recommended listing range, with confidence band.
- **A small map:** print + digital. Subject pin in copper, comps in grass, with date-and-price labels. Signals "we know where this is, geographically and temporally."
- **Print expansion:** add 2–4 more comps (5–7 total). The expanded comp set is the moat — competitors show 3.

## P8 · The Honest Assessment (digital P5; print P10–11)
**JTBD:** Inoculation. Name the weaknesses. Name them first.

**Layout:** stacked panels, each panel = one feature with two sentences (the trade-off) and one bold reframe sentence.

- 6–7 panels. Each panel begins with a structural fact ("658 m² is mid-sized for Merrimac"), explains the trade-off in dollar terms, and ends with a **bold value reframe**.
- Print adds one panel that has no analogue in v2: **Limits of our evidence** — what we couldn't see (interior smells, neighbour disputes, recent build defects), what an in-person inspection would change, how the range moves if reality differs from our remote read. The pratfall effect at work.

## P9 · Property Through Our Eyes (digital P4; print P12–14)
**JTBD:** Show that we have looked carefully, not just at data but at the home itself.

- **Hero photograph** (exterior, full-bleed in print).
- **Six room-condition cards** with /10 scores and 2-sentence prose. Precise materials named.
- **Print bonus spread:** the *Photography Audit* — we mark up two existing listing photos (when available from past sales of the property) and identify what a Fields shoot would change. Or, if no prior listing exists, a paired example from the suburb showing same-house-different-photography. This is the moment the seller realises we will *direct the photographer*, not just hire one.

# SECTION II — EVIDENCE (digital pp. 6–8; print pp. 15–24)

The data layer. The reasons behind the conclusions.

## P10 · Section divider — EVIDENCE
**JTBD:** Restful pause, switch to data mode.

- Full-bleed grass colour. *Evidence — every claim in this report can be traced to a source. Here are the sources.*

## P11 · Location & Lifestyle (digital P6; print P16–17)
**JTBD:** Local mastery proof. The seller realises we know their street.

**5-second:** Satellite tile + flood-status panel.
**60-second:** Setting + Street Position cards. Six POI cards with walking distances.
**Deep:** A paragraph of street-level prose: *"This pocket of cul-de-sacs off Highfield Drive is unusual in Merrimac — Kingary Wetland creates a permanent green boundary that cannot be built out, and the cul-de-sac configuration eliminates through-traffic. Across the last 36 months, eight homes have transacted within 800m, all at a premium to the wider Merrimac median…"*

- Flood panel is a separate framed callout — Merrimac sellers' #1 concern, and ours is the only report addressing it on page 1 of the location section.
- POIs are walking distances, named, categorised. We name the cafe, not "cafes nearby".

## P12 · The Buyer Pool (digital P7; print P18–19 spread)
**JTBD:** Specificity bias + narrative transportation. The seller understands *who* will pay them.

**5-second:** Three buyer-profile cards.
**60-second:** Each card has a name, a 3-sentence persona, a school/lifestyle anchor.
**Deep:** A paragraph imagining a buyer's morning in this home — the narrative-transportation device.

- **Print bonus:** one full page becomes "A Saturday morning in this home, told as a buyer would see it" — 200 words of pure scene-setting prose. This becomes one of the most-quoted pages.
- **Not Ideal For** panel — names the buyers this home is *not* for. Inoculation continued.

## P13 · Market Context (digital P7 callouts; print P20–21 spread)
**JTBD:** Seller learns more about their suburb than they have learned in years of living there.

- **Scarcity statistic** (e.g. "5 five-bedroom homes sold in Merrimac in 12 months, only 1 had a pool").
- **3 stat tiles:** median house price, houses sold (12m), currently listed.
- **Seasonality panel:** specific best-window for this suburb + this property type. (e.g. Robina = November peak; Burleigh = March; Varsity = April.)
- **Print expansion:** a small heatmap of monthly sale price index for the suburb (drawn from 13,585 GC sales). The reader sees *their* suburb, not a generic chart.
- **Demand-bracket map:** for the recommended listing range, how many active listings sit inside the same $X-to-$Y portal bracket. ("In the $1.75M–$2M Merrimac bracket, your listing would have zero direct competitors next weekend.")

## P14 · Risk + Protection Panel (print only; digital callouts)
**JTBD:** Pre-empt every objection an inspecting buyer will raise.

- **Flood:** council overlay status, AHD ground level, ICA insurance flood zone status, distance to historical flash-flood events.
- **School catchment:** named school, catchment status as of the current academic year, recent zoning notices.
- **Infrastructure:** any planned roads, council DAs within 500m, transport upgrades (positive or negative).
- **Body corporate / strata:** for units only.
- **Easements / encumbrances:** title-search level intelligence where available.

This page is the single most underrated competitive moat in our report. No competitor includes this. It is the antidote to the buyer's first three Google searches.

## P15 · Methodology + Sources (print P23; digital P12)
**JTBD:** Authority transfer, fluency mitigation.

- All citations listed in academic format.
- Adjustment-rate table for the suburb (full version, not the summary on P4).
- "What we explicitly did not model and why" — a 4-bullet list. Inoculation.

## P17 · How We Would Position & Sell — Pricing (digital P8; print P26–27 spread)
**JTBD:** Frame our pricing decisions in research, not opinion.

**5-second:** Four pricing-strategy cards (Aspirational / Competitive / Strategic / Floor) with ranges.
**60-second:** Each card has a one-sentence rationale anchored in this property's comp evidence.
**Deep:**
- *Why Precise Numbers, Not Round Figures* — Cardella & Seiler 2016, n=538.
- *Why List With a Range* — Nikiforou et al. 2022, n=538.
- *Private Treaty vs Auction* — Frino, Peat & Wright 2012, n=1.2M; REA 2014.
- *Getting the Price Right from Day One* — Taylor 1999; Anglin/Rutherford/Springer 2003; Knight 2002; Zillow 2019 (n=25,000).

The current v2 page 8 is already very close to this. Print version expands with one chart — the overpricing penalty curve from the seller book.

## P18 · How We Would Position & Sell — Features (digital P9; print P28)
**JTBD:** Show the seller exactly which features carry the campaign.

- 5–6 feature panels. Each: feature + dollar impact range + 2-sentence positioning strategy + photography note.
- Print adds: a *Pre-sale recommendations* sidebar — what the seller could do (or not do) before listing, with ROI estimates from our pre-sale ROI dataset. (Painting walls = $X spent / $Y recovered. Re-grouting = X / Y. Etc.)

## P19 · How We Would Position & Sell — Campaign (digital P10; print P29–30 spread)
**JTBD:** Show the campaign as a designed thing, not a generic checklist.

- **Campaign structure** (week-by-week timeline, illustrated as a Gantt-style strip).
- **Photography strategy** (twilight, drone for wetland reveal, dual-living zones colour-coded on floor plan).
- **Open home strategy** (peak-end rule applied; finishing at the pool deck).
- **Digital targeting** (named portal brackets + geo + audience layers).
- **Print bonus:** *The First 7 Days* — the demand-concentration insight from the seller book made specific to this campaign.
- **Print bonus:** *Active vs Passive Buyer Pool* — short explainer of why our marketing must reach both, with the chart from the seller book localised to this suburb's likely numbers.

## P20 · The Negotiation Plan (print only)
**JTBD:** Show that we have already thought about the offer-day moment.

- Three offer scenarios — early high (week 1), mid-range cluster (week 2–3), late low (week 4+) — with our recommended response framework for each.
- Specific to this property's likely buyer pool.
- This page is the kind of thinking sellers expect from a $50K consulting engagement, not a free appraisal. Including it is a deliberate over-deliver.

## P21 · The Outcome Projection (print only)
**JTBD:** Frame the financial reality of the choice ahead.

- Two scenarios shown side-by-side: *correctly priced day-one campaign* vs *overpriced + reduce-after-X-days*. Numbers drawn from the overpricing-penalty research and adapted to this property's range.
- Net-to-vendor figures for each, with marketing costs itemised.
- This is loss-aversion in action. The seller sees the dollar cost of *not getting it right*.

# THE LIVING MICROSITE — beyond the print

Same content, but with three layers no print can match.

### M1 · The Animated Cover
- 6–8 second twilight pan loop on landing.
- Subject address, prepared-for, date.
- One discreet "Listen to Will" play button → 90 second video summary of the report's verdict.

### M2 · Embedded Video
- Will speaks the verdict on camera (90 sec).
- Optional: a 4-minute deeper walkthrough where Will narrates over a screen-share of the report itself.
- Storage in `/voice-agent` infrastructure (existing).

### M3 · Live Comparable-Sales Feed
- New comparable sales surfaces here as they sell. Push-notification optional.
- Confidence-band widens or narrows live.
- Demand-bracket count updates weekly.
- This is the *Living* in Living Microsite — the seller comes back, and the report is different.

### M4 · "What We Got Wrong" Feedback
- One-tap form. Seller corrects any property fact (square metres, year of last renovation, etc.).
- Triggers a re-render of the affected pages within 24h. Auto-emails the updated PDF.
- Ben Franklin effect. The seller has now contributed; the report is partly theirs.

### M5 · Conversation Calendar
- Single button: "Book a 30-minute walk-through with Will." Live calendar.
- Auto-confirms via Telegram + Gmail (existing pipeline).
- The default ask is *a conversation about the report*, never a listing agreement.

### M6 · Engagement telemetry → Will's CRM
- Existing tracking-server captures every page-view, time-spent, download.
- Will sees real-time on Telegram. Conversation is informed by what the seller engaged with most.

# Cross-edition behaviours

### Tracking
- All three editions use the same `tracking_id`. Existing `email_tracking` collection.
- Telegram fires on: open, view, page-view-X, download, session-end.
- Microsite is the single canonical surface; print and PDF link back to it.

### Personalisation
Every report is bespoke to the address. Personalised at minimum:
- Cover photograph
- Address & client name on every page header
- Suburb-specific adjustment rates and stats
- Suburb-specific seasonality + best-window
- Property-specific buyer profiles
- Property-specific lifestyle narrative
- Property-specific photography brief
- Property-specific open-home routing
- A signed handwritten close from Will

The amount of "manual customisation" the seller perceives is far higher than the amount we actually do, because the data pipeline does the heavy lifting. This is the intended optical illusion.

### Audit-readiness
Every claim must trace to: (a) a comparable sale in `Gold_Coast.<suburb>`, (b) a stat in `system_monitor.precomputed_market_charts`, or (c) a citation in `01_psychology_principles.md` §8. If a sentence cannot be traced, it does not ship.

# What's NOT in the report

Calling out the void is part of the design.

- **No agent biography page.** A footnote line. That's it.
- **No agency awards.** Anywhere. We are pre-revenue.
- **No team page.** Will is the team.
- **No "we won the suburb" past-listings carousel.** When we have past sales, they live as comparable evidence in the data layer, not as marketing.
- **No commission discussion.** Belongs in the conversation, not the report.
- **No service-tier pricing.** Same.
- **No social-media quotes / press logos.** Borrowed authority is brittle.

If we add any of these later, we lose the discipline of the format.
