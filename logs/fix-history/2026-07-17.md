## [BRAIN2-LEADS] Native FB lead-form conversions invisible to Brain 2 — 11:15 AEST
**Symptom:** A FB lead-capture (Instant Form) launched 2026-07-16 produced 2 buyer leads shortly after going live, but they did not surface anywhere in the Brain 2 rollup (`ad_query.py`).
**Root cause:** Brain 2's attribution layer (`ad_downstream`, `organic_journeys`) is built entirely on PostHog *website sessions*; its "conversions" column counts on-site address-entry events. A Meta Instant Form converts ON Facebook — the person never lands on the site, so PostHog sees nothing. Compounding: `fb-lead-puller.py` stored only `field_data` and never captured `ad_id`, so leads were not even joinable to an ad; and no `scripts/brain2/` script read `fb_leads`.
**Fix:** (1) Enhanced `fb-lead-puller.py` to request+store `ad_id/adset_id/campaign_id/platform/is_organic` on each lead; backfilled the 3 existing leads from Meta (retained). (2) New `scripts/brain2/lead_attribution_build.py` → `system_monitor.lead_attribution` (per-lead + `_summary`), joins leads to ad spend, scores intent, splits buyer/seller-AYH/internal-test; nightly cron 23:52. (3) `ad_query.py`: `load()` now joins lead counts + surfaces `reach` and `post_engagement` (already collected, never shown); rollup header now separates on-site vs off-site conversions; added `ad_query.py leads` command. Result: last night's 2 leads (1 hot/Robina/"now", 1 owns-GC-home) now show at $0.54/lead under campaign "Leads: Buyer Brief — 5 that matter v1".
