## [EDITORIAL-ALWAYS-VERIFY] Last-attempt draft was accepted blind — now always verified — 02:55 AEST
**Ask:** Will asked what it'd take to fix the gap where "Draft 4, when the pipeline gives up, no verification pass runs at all — it's accepted blind."
**Root cause:** `_run_draft2_loop` only ran the verify step when `attempt < MAX_RETRIES` — on the final attempt it skipped straight to acceptance with no fact-check at all, conflating two genuinely different states under one vague "unverified_max_retries" tag: (a) the draft might actually be fine, we just never checked, or (b) it's genuinely still wrong and we ran out of budget to fix it.
**Fix:** Removed the `if attempt < MAX_RETRIES` gate around the verify call — it now runs unconditionally on every attempt, including the last. The attempt count now only controls whether there's budget left to RETRY (rewrite again), not whether the result gets CHECKED. Replaced the vague "unverified_max_retries" tag with three precise outcomes: "clean" (0 failures, any attempt including the last), "minor_flags" (within threshold), "max_retries_with_failures" (verified, genuinely still wrong, out of retries — a confirmed fact now, not a guess). Auto-publish gating (from the previous fix) needed no logic change — max_retries_with_failures is still correctly excluded — just an updated comment.
**Side benefit:** a last-attempt draft that happens to actually be clean now correctly auto-publishes instead of being needlessly held back under the old blanket "unverified" label. Adds one extra Verify call in the (roughly 1-in-3 to 1-in-2, per today's testing) case a property needs all 3 draft2 attempts — modest cost (~$0.02-0.10/call at current cached rates).
**Verified:** unit-tested store_analysis's gating against all 4 outcomes (clean/minor_flags/max_retries_with_failures/failed_factcheck) with a mocked DB — behavior unchanged from the prior fix except the renamed/more-precise middle tag.
**Files:** scripts/backend_enrichment/generate_property_ai_analysis.py
