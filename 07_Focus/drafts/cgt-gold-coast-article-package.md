### Section 1: The Main Residence Exemption (What Most People Get Right)
- Your primary home is generally CGT-free under the main residence exemption
- Conditions: you must have lived in it, not used it to produce income, land must be under 2 hectares
- Data point: percentage of Gold Coast sales that are owner-occupied vs investor (cite ABS/CoreLogic if available)

### Section 2: The 6-Year Absence Rule (What Most People Get Wrong)
- If you move out and rent your home, you can maintain the exemption for up to 6 years
- Conditions: you cannot claim another property as your main residence during this period
- Common Gold Coast scenario: owner moves interstate, rents the property, returns within 6 years
- Data point: average hold period for Gold Coast properties that re-sell (from our sold data)

### Section 3: The 50% CGT Discount (What Changes After 12 Months)
- Individuals who hold for more than 12 months get a 50% discount on the capital gain
- Plain-English example using a real Gold Coast suburb median:
  - Bought in Robina 2020 at $950,000, sold 2026 at $1,490,000
  - Capital gain: $540,000. After 50% discount: $270,000 added to taxable income
  - At marginal rate of 37%: ~$99,900 in CGT (approximate, depends on total income)
- Data point: median price growth per suburb over 5 years from our database

### Section 4: Renovation and Improvement Costs (What Reduces the Gain)
- Cost base includes purchase price + stamp duty + legal fees + capital improvements
- Capital improvements reduce the taxable gain (new kitchen, extension, pool)
- Repairs and maintenance do NOT reduce the gain
- Common confusion: replacing an old kitchen (capital improvement) vs fixing a broken tap (repair)

### Section 5: What the Data Shows About Hold Periods on the Gold Coast
- Average hold period for sellers in Robina, Varsity Lakes, Burleigh Waters
- Distribution chart: how many sellers are inside vs outside the 12-month discount window
- Observation: very few Gold Coast sellers are selling inside 12 months (data-driven, no advice)
