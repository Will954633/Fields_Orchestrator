## The Principle

Roald Amundsen reached the South Pole and returned alive. Robert Scott didn't. The difference wasn't talent or resources — it was discipline. Amundsen marched 15-20 miles every day, good weather or bad. Scott pushed hard on good days and rested on bad days. Amundsen's consistency meant he always knew where he'd be and when he'd arrive. Scott was always guessing.

**Fields operates the same way.** Each day has a checkpoint — a defined, achievable outcome. Hit it every day. Don't overreach on high-energy days (that's where novelty chasing lives). Don't skip on low-energy days (that's where grind avoidance lives). The checkpoints are set by the milestone dates, and the milestone dates are set by Q3.

## Weekly Sprint Structure

Each week has 5 working days (Mon-Fri). Saturday is optional catch-up. Sunday is off.

### Time Blocks Per Day

| Block | Duration | Purpose |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Morning checkpoint** (7:00 AM) | 15 min | AI presents today's checkpoint + grind task. Will confirms or adjusts. |
| **Deep work block** | 2-3 hours | The main checkpoint task. No interruptions, no tangents. |
| **Grind block** | 30-60 min | One accounting/email/admin task. Scheduled, not optional. |
| **Content block** | 30-60 min | Review content briefs, record video, approve posts. |
| **End-of-day log** | 10 min | Did we hit the checkpoint? What blocked us? What's tomorrow? |

**Total Will time: ~4-5 focused hours/day.** The rest is AI executing in parallel (building, deploying, researching, drafting).

### Weekly Rhythm

| Day | Deep Work Focus | Grind Focus | Content Focus |
|-----|----------------|-------------|---------------|
| **Monday** | Sprint planning + main build task | Email triage (catch up from weekend) | Review weekly content brief |
| **Tuesday** | Main build task (continued) | Accounting task (invoices, reconciliation) | Record video batch |
| **Wednesday** | Main build task or review AI output | Email follow-ups | Approve queued posts |
| **Thursday** | Secondary task or iteration | Expense tracking / API spend review | Record video (if needed) |
| **Friday** | Sprint review + next week prep | Admin catch-up | Review engagement report |

### Checkpoint Rules

1. **One primary checkpoint per day.** Not three. Not "try to get to." ONE thing that must be done.
2. **The checkpoint is achievable.** If it takes more than one day, it was scoped wrong. Break it down.
3. **AI does prep work before the checkpoint.** If today's checkpoint is "review Decision Feed with real data," AI has already wired the backend before Will sits down.
4. **Grind task is non-negotiable.** Even 30 minutes. It's in the checkpoint. It gets done.
5. **If the checkpoint isn't hit, log why.** Not shame — data. The pattern of misses reveals what's actually hard vs what's being avoided.
6. **If Will works on something off-plan, log it.** "Explored [X] for 2h instead of checkpoint. Reason: [felt important / got distracted / was actually urgent]." Over 4 weeks this data is gold.

### The Three Buckets

Every CEO agent proposal gets triaged into one of three buckets:

| Bucket | Rule | What Happens |
|--------|------|-------------|
| **AI DOING NOW** | Engineering/data quality tasks that don't need Will's input, align with current sprint, and are high confidence | AI picks these up and executes during the session. Will sees results in end-of-day log. |
| **NEEDS YOUR APPROVAL** | Budget decisions, ad changes, product direction, anything touching live website or public-facing content | Presented to Will with the agent's reasoning. Will approves, defers, or rejects. Approved items get executed immediately or queued for today. |
| **NOTED FOR LATER** | Low/medium priority, not aligned with current sprint theme, or depends on something not yet done | Logged. Resurfaces when its sprint arrives or its dependency clears. |

### Triage Rules

How AI decides which bucket:

```
Is it high confidence + engineering/data quality?
  AND does it align with the current sprint theme?
  AND does it NOT touch budget, ads, live website, or public content?
    → AI DOING NOW

Does it require a budget decision?
  OR does it change live ads/website?
  OR does it change product direction?
  OR is confidence < high?
    → NEEDS YOUR APPROVAL

Is it low/medium priority?
  OR is it outside the current sprint's theme?
  OR does it depend on unfinished work?
    → NOTED FOR LATER
```

### Keeping the Agents Informed — The Feedback Loop

The agents are only as useful as the context they receive. Right now they see OPS_STATUS, fix history, metrics, and config. They DON'T see: the sprint plan, what Will actually worked on, which proposals were approved or rejected, milestone progress, or the Q3 deadline. That makes their proposals generic.

**The goal:** The agents should reason about how to propel us toward the next milestone while maintaining everything that needs maintaining. They can do a lot — but only if they know where we're going.

#### Maintenance vs Milestone Balance

The agents must maintain the operational foundation (pipeline, data quality, ad performance) while also driving toward milestones. The rule:

**Maintenance is the floor. Milestones are the ceiling.**

```
Agent time allocation guidance:
├── 30% Maintenance — keep pipeline healthy, data fresh, ads running,
│                     coverage complete. This is non-negotiable baseline.
├── 50% Milestone support — proposals that directly advance the current
│                           sprint/milestone. This is the main job.
└── 20% Next-sprint prep — identify blockers and pre-build for the
                           next sprint so transitions are smooth.
```

Chief of Staff enforces this balance. If Engineering spends 80% of proposals on pipeline tweaks and 20% on milestone work, Chief of Staff flags it: "Engineering is over-indexing on maintenance. Sprint 2 needs Decision Feed backend — redirect engineering focus."

> (a) unlock a future action earlier than planned
> (b) de-risk an upcoming sprint
> (c) increase the likelihood of hitting a milestone
> (d) eliminate a dependency before it becomes a blocker"

**This is the real leverage of AI planning.** A human can execute today's checkpoint. AI can execute today's checkpoint AND pre-solve problems 4 weeks out simultaneously.

**How it works:**

Every day, alongside the morning checkpoint analysis, the AI scans `sprints/sprint-01-to-12` and produces:

```

  → Content gap analysis needed. I can run search-intent-analyser.py
    today and have the gap list ready 2 weeks early.
```

**Agent directive for look-ahead:**

Chief of Staff gets this standing order: "After producing today's top 3, scan Sprints 2-6. Identify any task that: (a) has a long lead time and should start now, (b) depends on something that isn't yet in progress, (c) could fail and needs a de-risk action, or (d) could be pulled forward to accelerate the timeline. Report these as 'LOOK-AHEAD' items in your daily brief."

Engineering, Product, and Growth agents get: "For each proposal you make, check if it pre-solves any problem in Sprints 2-6. If it does, note which sprint and milestone it accelerates. Proposals that serve both today AND next month are highest value."

## The 4-Week Test

This framework runs for 4 weeks (Sprints 1-4). At the end of Sprint 4 (April 25), we review:

1. **Did the checkpoints help?** Did Will feel more focused? Did more get done?
2. **Were checkpoints achievable?** Or consistently too ambitious / too easy?
3. **Did grind blocks work?** Is the accounting backlog shrinking?
4. **Did content launch happen?** Are posts going out, videos recorded?
5. **Are leads coming in?** Even 1-2 leads = the funnel is working.

If yes → continue with Sprints 5-12.
If no → diagnose what failed and adjust. The framework serves Will, not the other way around.

## The Non-Negotiables

Across all 12 weeks, regardless of what sprint we're in:

1. **Hit the checkpoint.** Every day. Good day or bad day.
2. **Do the grind task.** Even 30 minutes. Non-negotiable.
3. **Don't skip content.** The audience compounds. A week of silence = lost momentum.
4. **Push to GitHub.** Every change. Every day. Disaster recovery is not optional.
5. **Log everything.** Checkpoints hit/missed, diversions, blockers. The data is how we improve.
