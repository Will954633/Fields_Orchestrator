# Weekly Content Playbook — Facebook Organic

> **Date:** 2026-03-30
> **Status:** DRAFT — Will to refine and confirm
> **Key shift:** Automated buyer-content posts are dead. Nobody clicked, Facebook couldn't find an audience. New strategy: Will on camera + engagement-driven formats that get comments and shares.

---

## Why This Changes Everything

The old schedule (15 automated templates, 2x/day) was content nobody asked for, delivered by a brand nobody knew yet. The missing ingredient is **Will**. People follow people, not data platforms. A face on camera saying "look at this" in 40 seconds creates a relationship that no automated data card ever will.

This is also Kara's hypothesis made real: **digital warm-up reduces cold-call friction.** If buyers see Will 3-4 times a week talking about their suburb, they feel like they know him before he ever calls. That's the moat.

## Weekly Content Lineup (10-12 posts/week)

### Data Videos (2x/week) — Will on camera, 40-60 seconds

Short, punchy, one concept per video. Will talks to camera with data overlaid or shown on screen.

**Format:** Film on phone, vertical (Reels/Stories format), subtitle overlays, Fields branding lower-third.

**Content types to rotate and test:**

| Type | Example | Why It Works |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **Median price walkthrough** | Will walks through a home at median price: "This is what $1,250,000 actually gets you in Robina right now" | People have no idea what median looks like. Visual + number = sticky. |
| **Single stat explainer** | "Burleigh Waters had 38% more sales this quarter vs last. Here's why that matters." | One number, one insight, 40 seconds. Shareable. |
| **Buyer vs asking price** | "What buyers actually paid vs asking price this month — the gap might surprise you" | Tension between ask and reality. Gets comments. |
| **Price per sqm comparison** | "You can get 40% more floor space in Varsity Lakes vs Burleigh for the same money" | Practical, comparison-driven, suburb rivalry. |
| **Days on market trends** | "Homes in Robina are selling 8 days faster than 3 months ago. What's driving it?" | Urgency signal. Sellers share this. |
| **New listing first look** | "Brand new to market — here's my first take on this one" | Freshness, authority, shows Will sees everything first. |

**AI role:**
- Identify the most interesting data point each week from pipeline data
- Write 40-60 second transcript draft
- Monitor which video types get most engagement → feed back into next week's picks
- Track: views, comments, shares, saves, click-throughs per content type

**Will's role:** Record the video. Can be casual — phone, natural light, real locations. Authenticity > production value.

---

### Question / Opinion Posts (2x/week) — High engagement, comment drivers

These exist to get people talking. Comments = algorithmic boost = reach.

| Type | Example | Why It Works |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **"Would you pay X for this?"** | Property photo + price. "Would you pay $1,450,000 for this? Tell me in the comments." | Low friction opinion. Everyone has a view on price. Gets arguments going. |
| **"Would you rather?"** | Side-by-side: "Would you rather: this renovated kitchen in Varsity ($1.2M) or this bigger block in Mudgeeraba ($1.1M)?" | Trade-off framing. Uses our valuation + analysis data to pick genuinely interesting comparisons. |
| **"Which suburb wins?"** | "For a family with $1.5M — Robina or Burleigh Waters? Here's what each gets you." | Suburb tribalism. People defend their area. |
| **"Guess the price"** | Property photo, features listed, no price. "What do you think this sold for? Answer before you scroll." | Gamification. High engagement. Reveal in comments or follow-up post. |
| **"Hot take"** | "Unpopular opinion: renovated kitchens add less value than most sellers think. Here's the data." | Provocation + data = engagement. |

**AI role:**
- Select property pairs for "would you rather" using valuation data and AI analysis (different features, similar price = best comparisons)
- Generate comparison grids: 4 key differences between two properties
- Draft post copy (Will adjusts tone)
- Pull actual sold price data for "guess the price" reveals

**Will's role:** Pick the posts that feel right for the week. Add personal takes. Respond to comments (this is where relationships form).

### Property Story Posts (1-2x/week) — Narrative-driven, proven format

These already work in paid ads ($0.16/LPV). Bring them organic.

| Type | Example | Why It Works |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **"This one will surprise you"** | A property that looks ordinary but has hidden value (or vice versa) | Curiosity gap. Proven in ads. |
| **"Worst house, best street"** | Properties in premium locations with below-average condition | Everyone loves this format. Classic real estate narrative. |
| **"Why this home will sell first — 3 reasons"** | Data-backed prediction on a current listing | Shows authority. People check back to see if Will was right. |
| **"Someone bought this X months ago. Now it's back."** | Already our top-performing ad creative | Story tension. Why is it back? What happened? |
| **"The most unusual home in [suburb] right now"** | Properties with unique features, rare layouts, interesting history | Shareability. "Have you seen this?" factor. |
| **Flip/renovation tracking** | "This sold for $850K in 2024. They spent ~$200K renovating. Now listed at $1.35M. Worth it?" | Story + data + opinion prompt. |

**AI role:**
- Scan new and existing listings weekly for story candidates using AI analysis data
- Flag: properties with high value_gap_pct (overpriced/underpriced), unusual features, short re-list cycles, outlier condition scores
- Draft story narrative (Will adjusts)
- Cross-reference with property articles already generated

**Will's role:** Choose which stories to run. Add personal perspective. These can be video or image+text.

### Article Posts (1-2x/week) — Promote what's already working

Only promote articles that are performing well in ads this week. Don't push dead content.

| Type | Example |
|------|---------|
| **Top-performing article** | "This article got 63 clicks this week — [title]. If you haven't read it yet: [link]" |
| **Data article teaser** | Pull one striking stat from an article, post as standalone, link for full analysis |
| **Article series** | "Part 2 of our Robina deep dive is live. Part 1 had 400 readers — here's what they learned." |

**AI role:**
- Weekly scan: which articles got most engagement (from `article_events` + ad metrics)?
- Draft promotional post copy
- Suggest which articles to boost with ad spend

**Will's role:** Approve posts. Add "here's why I wrote this" framing.

One post per week that has nothing to do with property. Builds the "local" brand.

| Type | Example |

**AI role:** Remind Will to take/share a photo. Can't generate this content — it has to be real.

**Will's role:** This is 100% Will. Phone photo + 1-2 sentences. The more casual, the better.

### Personal Insight Video (1x/week) — Will's perspective, longer form

60-90 seconds. Will sharing a thought about the market, the business, or what he's learning. Not data — opinion and personality.

| Type | Example |
|------|---------|
| **"What I learned this week"** | "I looked at 50 valuations this week. Here's the one pattern I keep seeing." |
| **"Why I started Fields"** | Origin story. Run variations quarterly. |
| **Behind the scenes** | "This is what my Monday looks like running a one-person real estate data company." |
| **Market commentary** | "Everyone's asking me about interest rates. Here's what I actually think matters more." |
| **Honest take** | "I got this valuation wrong last month. Here's what I missed and what it taught me." |

**AI role:**
- Suggest weekly topic based on: what data was interesting, what questions came in, what trends are emerging
- Draft talking points (not a script — bullet points Will can riff from)
- Monitor engagement: which personal content types resonate?

**Will's role:** Record and post. Be real. Vulnerability and honesty outperform polish.

AI monitors post performance daily and stores in `system_monitor.content_performance`:
- Views, likes, comments, shares, saves per post
- Click-throughs to website
- Engagement rate by content type
- Comment sentiment (positive/negative/questions)

### Weekly Review (Friday/Saturday)
AI generates engagement summary:
- Which content types performed best/worst
- Trending up or down vs prior weeks
- Recommended adjustments for next week
- New content type experiments to try

### Feedback Loop
Over 4-8 weeks, the system builds a performance profile:
- "Data videos average 340 views, question posts average 23 comments, property stories average 8 shares"
- This data informs: more of what works, less of what doesn't, and new experiments for untested formats
- Feeds into marketing-advisor.py for cross-channel optimisation

## Video Production (Keeping It Simple)

Will doesn't need a studio. The winning formula for short-form real estate video:

**Equipment:** Phone (vertical), natural light, no tripod needed
**Location:** In front of properties, at desk with screen showing data, walking through suburbs
**Duration:** 40-60 seconds (data), 60-90 seconds (personal insight)
**Editing:** Minimal — subtitle overlays, Fields logo lower-third. Can be done in CapCut or similar in 5 minutes.
**Posting:** Direct to Facebook page (native video gets 10x reach vs links)

**AI can help with:**
- Auto-generating subtitle files from transcript
- Creating data overlay graphics (charts, numbers) for screen recording
- Suggesting B-roll shots ("film the kitchen at 14 Willow Ct for the median price walkthrough")

**AI cannot help with:**
- Being on camera. That's Will. And that's the point.

## Content Production Pipeline — Always 1-2 Weeks Ahead

The rule: **Will never sits down to record without a fully prepared brief.** When it's time to film, the research is done, the transcript is drafted, the comparison data is pulled, the Facebook post copy is written, and the creative assets are ready. Will's only job is to perform.

### The Two-Week Rolling Buffer

```
         WEEK -2              WEEK -1              THIS WEEK
     ┌──────────────┐    ┌──────────────┐    ┌──────────────┐
     │  AI RESEARCH  │    │  AI DRAFTS   │    │ WILL RECORDS │
     │  & PLANNING   │───▶│  & PREPARES  │───▶│  & POSTS     │
     │               │    │              │    │              │
     │ • Scan data   │    │ • Transcripts│    │ • Film videos│
     │ • Pick topics │    │ • Post copy  │    │ • Review copy│
     │ • Flag stories│    │ • Creatives  │    │ • Approve    │
     │ • Pull comps  │    │ • Schedule   │    │ • Post/queue │
     └──────────────┘    └──────────────┘    └──────────────┘
```

At any point, there should be:
- **This week:** Content ready to post. Will records videos from prepared briefs, approves queued posts.
- **Next week:** Fully drafted. Transcripts, post copy, property photos selected, comparison data pulled. Waiting for Will's video recordings.
- **Week after:** Topics identified, research started. AI is pulling data and flagging stories.

### Weekly Production Cadence

#### Saturday/Sunday — AI Generates Next Week's Full Brief

AI runs automatically (or on request) and produces:

**`system_monitor.content_briefs`** — one document per week containing:
- All 10-11 post deliverables as described above
- Property photos selected and linked
- All data points verified against live pipeline
- Post copy ready to paste
- Video transcripts ready to read

**Delivered via:** Telegram summary + full brief accessible in chat agent

#### Monday — Will Reviews Brief

- 20-30 minute review session
- Approve, adjust, or swap topics
- Flag any videos that need site visits (plan filming around schedule)

#### Tuesday-Thursday — Will Records Videos

- 3-4 videos in one batch session if possible (data video x2, personal insight x1, optional property story video)
- Each video has a prepared brief waiting — just open it and record
- Total filming time: ~30 minutes for a week of video content

#### Ongoing — Will Approves Queued Posts

- AI queues non-video posts (question/opinion, article promo, text-based property stories) in a review queue
- Will reviews in chat agent or directly, approves or edits
- Approved posts are scheduled via `fb-content-scheduler.py` (repurposed)

#### Friday — AI Generates Engagement Report + Starts Next Week Research

- How did this week's content perform?
- Which content types drove most comments/shares/clicks?
- Begin research for the week after next (maintaining the 2-week buffer)
