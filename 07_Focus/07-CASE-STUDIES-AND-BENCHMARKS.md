## Case Study 1: Noah Escobar — YouTube to $16M in Sales

**Source:** Inman (April 2025) — [inman.com](https://www.inman.com/2025/04/24/how-to-leverage-youtube-to-go-from-contact-to-contract-in-17-days/)
**Found by:** Chief of Staff Agent

**Problem:** How to generate warm buyer leads without cold prospecting in a local real estate market.

**Solution:** Built YouTube as the primary inbound engine using a **three-video stack per neighbourhood:**
1. **Infrastructure videos** — what's being built, what's changing, development updates
2. **Community overview videos** — lifestyle, schools, amenities, who lives there ("Living In" format)
3. **Listing tour videos** — specific property walkthroughs with market context

This stack dominated search for specific neighbourhoods. Not generic "real estate tips" — hyper-local search ownership.

**Result:** Over 12 months:
- $16 million in sales sourced directly from YouTube
- **17 days average from first contact to under contract**
- Days with 5 inbound leads from YouTube
- Two properties under contract within 3 days of first contact
- Leads arrived pre-warmed: they'd watched multiple videos before reaching out

**What this changes for Fields:**

1. **YouTube video sequencing.** Our plan already has "Living In" guides and market data videos. Escobar's model adds a critical third pillar: **infrastructure/change videos** ("What's happening in Robina right now — 3 developments that will affect prices"). We should add this to the video ideas.

2. **Three-video stack per suburb.** Instead of randomly rotating pillars, each suburb gets a deliberate stack:
   - "Living in Robina" (community overview)
   - "What's Changing in Robina in 2026" (infrastructure/development)
   - "Best Value Home in Robina Right Now" (listing tour/story)
   When all three rank, that suburb is owned.

3. **17-day contact-to-contract timeline.** This proves the digital warm-up hypothesis. Leads who've watched 10+ minutes of content don't need convincing — they've already decided they trust the agent. This should be Fields' target benchmark.

4. **Suburb-specific CTAs on every video.** Each video links to the corresponding suburb page on fieldsestate.com.au. Not a generic "visit our website" — "See live data for Robina at fieldsestate.com.au/market-metrics/robina."

**Sprint impact:** Refines YouTube strategy (Sprint 5-6). First 3 videos should complete the Robina stack, not spread across suburbs.

## Case Study 2: Realtor.com — Reducing Browse-to-Action Gap

**Source:** Realtor.com product blog — [realtor.com](https://www.realtor.com/homemade/realtor-com-app-the-home-of-home-search-wherever-you-go/)
**Supporting data:** Zillow Buyer Trends Report 2025 — [zillow.com](https://www.zillow.com/research/buyers-housing-trends-report-2025-35688/)
**Found by:** Product Agent

**Problem:** Property search products create attention but not enough movement from browsing to action. People look at listings but don't contact agents.

**Solution:** Realtor.com tested in-app flows that let users:
- **Schedule tours** directly from the listing
- **Contact a matched agent** with one tap
- **Save and track** properties with price change notifications
- Reduced the gap between discovery and action to near-zero friction

**Result:** Test markets saw:
- Consumer satisfaction improve 20-120%
- Engagement with agents improve 20-120%
- Close rates improve 20-120%

**Supporting data from Zillow:** Contacting an agent is now the **most common first homebuying step** (not searching online). Most buyers install a real estate app before other steps.

**What this changes for Fields:**

1. **"Track this property" is the killer feature.** Not a PDF download, not a newsletter signup. The highest-converting capture mechanism is: "Want to know when this property's price changes? Enter your email." It's utility, not marketing.

2. **"Track this suburb" is the second feature.** Weekly digest for a specific suburb. Not "subscribe to our newsletter" — "Get Robina's weekly market update." Specific, useful, low commitment.

3. **"Request a valuation review" is the seller equivalent.** Not "Analyse Your Home" (too vague) — "Get a free valuation review comparing your home to recent sales." More specific, more actionable.

4. **In-context actions, not separate landing pages.** The CTA should be ON the property page, ON the Decision Feed card, ON the market-metrics page — not requiring navigation to a separate form.

**Sprint impact:** Reframes Sprint 1 lead capture. "Track this property" (price alert) should be the PRIMARY capture mechanism, not just one of three.

## Case Study 3: Magri Cabinets — Zero to 3-5 Leads/Week

**Source:** DoubleBricks — [doublebricks.com](https://www.doublebricks.com/case-study/magri-cabinets-building-an-online-presence-from-ground-up)
**Found by:** Growth Agent

**Problem:** Local services business with zero online presence, no website, no structured lead generation. Relied entirely on word of mouth.

**Solution:**
1. Keyword research to identify high-intent searches
2. Competitor analysis
3. **Very small number of high-intent offer pages** (not a content farm)
4. Clear measurement on each page
5. Limited-budget Google Ads campaign on high-intent searches only

**Result:**
- Zero to 3-5 leads per week
- 20+ phone calls per month
- Built from nothing on a limited budget

**What this changes for Fields:**

1. **Small number of high-intent pages > many content pages.** The lesson isn't "publish more." It's "build 3-4 pages that answer the exact question someone has when they're ready to act, then drive all traffic there."

2. **For Fields, those pages are:**
   - `/analyse-your-home` — seller intent ("What's my property worth?")
   - Property pages with price alerts — buyer intent ("Is this property good value?")
   - `/market-metrics/[suburb]` — research intent ("What's happening in my suburb?")
   - Decision Feed — discovery intent ("Show me the best deals")

3. **Measurement on each page.** Every high-intent page needs its own conversion metric. Not just "traffic" — "of people who land on Analyse Your Home, what % submit the form?"

4. **Limited budget, high intent.** Mirrors Fields' situation exactly. Don't spray $3K/month across 20 ad variants. Concentrate on the 3-4 ads that drive people to the 3-4 high-intent pages.

**Sprint impact:** Reinforces the "prove one funnel path" recommendation. Sprint 1-2 should focus on making 2-3 pages convert, not building 10 surfaces.

## Case Study 4: Unbounce — Real Estate Landing Page Benchmarks

**Source:** Unbounce Landing Page Report — [unbounce.com](https://unbounce.com/landing-pages/high-converting-real-estate-landing-page-design/)
**Found by:** Data Quality + Engineering Agents (independently found the same study)

**Problem:** Real estate landing pages typically underconvert because they're too long, too complex, and written at too high a reading level.

**Benchmarks:**
- **Longer pages convert 33% worse** than shorter pages
- **6th-grade reading level copy converts nearly 2x** compared to university-level copy
- **8.7% conversion = outperforming 90% of real estate peers**
- **11.2% = highest-converting real estate landing pages**
- Pages that lead with one clear offer above the fold significantly outperform multi-offer pages

**What this changes for Fields:**

1. **Analyse Your Home page must be SHORT.** One headline, one subhead, one form, one button. Not an explanation of methodology, not a description of the business, not three paragraphs about data quality. "What's your home worth? Enter your address. We'll analyse it free."

2. **Price alert capture must be ONE field.** Email address. That's it. Not name + email + phone + suburb preference. One field, one button.

3. **Market report gate should be minimal.** "Enter your email to download" — not a 5-field form.

4. **Decision Feed CTA should be 6th-grade language.** Not "Receive personalised market intelligence" — "Want this analysis for your home? We'll do it free."

5. **Target benchmark: 5-8% conversion on dedicated capture pages.** If we're below 3%, the page is wrong, not the traffic.

**Sprint impact:** Directly changes Sprint 1-3 build specs. Every capture form should be reviewed against these benchmarks before launch.

## How to Use These Case Studies

### In Sprint Planning
Every sprint's capture/product work should reference at least one of these studies. Before building a new page or feature, ask: "Does this align with what Realtor.com, Unbounce, or Magri Cabinets found?"

### In CEO Agent Context
These case studies should be exported to the agents so they can reference them in future proposals. Add to `context/focus/case_studies.md` in the daily export.

### In Content Strategy
Noah Escobar's three-video stack should replace the generic "rotate pillars" YouTube approach. Each suburb gets a deliberate stack designed for search ownership.

### In Design Reviews
Unbounce benchmarks (short, simple, 6th-grade, one CTA) should be the design constraint for every capture page. Print and tape to wall equivalent.
