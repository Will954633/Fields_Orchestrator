## Will's Notes from Books (Wilkey & Carr)

These are Will's takeaways from reading both YouTube strategy books. They are the design constraints for everything below.

1. **Tell people what to do.** Put the call to action at the beginning of the video, not the end.
2. **Negative sells.** "5 things you need to know before buying in Robina" outperforms "Why Robina is great."
3. **Have the hook in the ad.** You have to tell people to call you.
4. **Include social proof in the hook.** E.g., "People call us all the time for guidance on how to position their home in the current market."
5. **Evergreen content.** "Living in Robina in 2026" — titles that stay relevant for years.
6. **Never run paid ads to YouTube videos.** Paid traffic ruins the organic tracking algorithm and destroys your ranking. Facebook/Google ads should NOT send people to YouTube.
7. **Reserve a key data point for later.** Tell viewers a key insight is coming but hold it back — keeps them watching. Can be subtle.
8. **To win at YouTube:** Right videos + right keywords + right thumbnails + right descriptions + right content + right calls to action + right hooks. Do all of that and you get the business.
9. **Don't post too often.** You end up competing with your own content.
10. **"I would never have door knocked as an agent. I would have gone hard on YouTube from the start."**
11. **Lighting matters.** Need a halo ring light for indoor filming.
12. **Competition and keywords matter.** Assess competition, dominate the right keywords. Need a keyword strategy where you choose where to play.
13. **The 6-7 minute view mark is critical for the YouTube algorithm.** When you build lots of views at the 6-7 minute mark, you start moving up rankings. Average view times around 10-11 minutes is where you break out. Need consistent, long, engaging videos.
14. **Most popular video subjects:** "Living in X city", "5 things I love and hate about X city", showcase lifestyle of an area.
15. **B-roll is essential.** B-roll = supplementary footage that plays while you're talking. If you say "Robina is 800m from the Town Centre," the viewer sees footage of the Town Centre, not your face. Phone footage of driving through the suburb, the shops, the lake, the parks. It's what makes the difference between a talking head and a mini-documentary.

### Why YouTube (Not Just Facebook)
- Facebook posts disappear in 24-48 hours. YouTube videos rank for years. (Point 5: evergreen)

- Compounding returns: one good video can generate leads for years. Facebook requires constant spend.
- Builds deeper trust than any other medium. 10 minutes of Will talking about a suburb = they feel like they know him.
- Kara's hypothesis: digital warm-up → warm leads instead of cold calls. YouTube is the strongest version of this.

### Why Not Yet (Dependencies)
- YouTube requires consistent, quality content. Can't half-do it.
- Need Facebook content rhythm established first (Phase A of battle plan — 2-3 weeks)
- Need basic lead capture on website (property page alerts, Analyse Your Home)
- Need lighting setup (halo ring)
- YouTube is a medium-term play (3-6 months to see results). Facebook gives faster signal.

**Recommended launch:** Late April / early May 2026 — after Facebook content rhythm is proven and lead capture is live.

## Content Pillars (4 Types)

### Pillar 1: "Living In" Suburb Guides (Evergreen, 10-15 min)

The highest-volume YouTube search category for real estate. These are the pillar videos that rank and generate leads for years.

**Format:** Will on camera + heavy B-roll of the suburb. Walking through streets, showing shops, parks, schools, water, transport. Data overlays for key stats.

**Target keywords (from our data):**
- "living in robina" / "living in robina 2026"
- "living in burleigh waters gold coast"
- "living in varsity lakes"
- "moving to gold coast 2026"
- "best suburbs gold coast families"
- "gold coast suburbs to avoid" (negative sells — point 2)
- "living in mudgeeraba" / "living in reedy creek"

**Template structure (12-15 minutes):**
```
0:00  Hook + CTA (point 1: tell them what to do at the start)
      "If you're thinking about moving to Robina, this video covers
       everything — prices, lifestyle, schools, and the one thing
       most people get wrong about this suburb. If you want a
       personalised suburb comparison, link below."
      Social proof: "We help people navigate the Gold Coast market
       every week — here's what we've learned about Robina."

0:30  Overview — what is this suburb? Where is it? Who lives here?
      [B-roll: aerial/driving footage, map overlay]

2:00  Lifestyle — what does daily life look like?
      [B-roll: Town Centre, cafes, lake, parks, transport]

4:00  Property market — what does your money get you?
      Median price, typical homes, price ranges by property type
      [B-roll: street-level footage of different property types]

6:00  The data deep-dive (this is where 6-7 min retention matters)
      Price trends, days on market, sales volume
      "Here's the number I promised you..." (point 7: reserved data point)
      [Screen share or overlay: Fields charts]

8:00  Schools, transport, amenities — the practical stuff
      [B-roll: school frontages, train station, highway access]

10:00 What most people get wrong (negative angle — point 2)
      "The 3 things nobody tells you about living in Robina"
      Honest trade-offs (traffic, flood zones, noise corridors)

12:00 Who should live here (and who shouldn't)
      Profiles: families, investors, downsizers, first-home buyers

13:00 CTA + outro
      "If you want to know what your money gets you in Robina vs
       Burleigh or Varsity, we've built a free comparison tool at
       fieldsestate.com.au. Link below."
```

**Production schedule:** 1 suburb guide per month. 6 suburbs = 6 months of pillar content.
**AI role:** Research suburb data, write full script draft, identify B-roll shot list, prepare data overlays.

## Integration with Facebook Content

YouTube and Facebook serve different purposes in the funnel:

| | Facebook | YouTube |
|---|---------|---------|
| **Content type** | Short (40-60s video, image posts) | Long (8-15 min video) |
| **Discovery** | Algorithm pushes to followers + similar | Search-driven — people find you |
| **Lifespan** | 24-48 hours | Years |
| **Trust depth** | "I've seen this guy" | "I know this guy" |
| **Lead warmth** | Warm-ish | Hot |
| **Effort per piece** | 10-15 min | 1-2 hours |

**Cross-promotion strategy:**
- YouTube videos get cut into 40-60s clips for Facebook (data videos)
- Facebook engagement data informs YouTube topics ("Would you pay?" posts that get 50+ comments = video topic)
- Both drive to same lead capture (fieldsestate.com.au property pages + Analyse Your Home)
- Facebook builds quick reach, YouTube builds deep trust. Together they compress the know → like → trust timeline.

**Content recycling:**
- Film B-roll once per suburb → use in YouTube "Living In" + Facebook lifestyle posts
- Data research for YouTube script → also feeds Facebook data video transcripts
- Property stories work in both formats (short for Facebook, long for YouTube)

## What NOT to Do (From the Books + Common Mistakes)

1. **Don't run paid ads to YouTube videos** (point 6) — ruins organic algorithm
2. **Don't post daily** (point 9) — you compete with yourself
3. **Don't make sales pitches** — make educational content that positions you as the expert
4. **Don't chase trending formats** — stick to evergreen search-intent content
5. **Don't neglect thumbnails** — a great video with a bad thumbnail gets zero clicks
6. **Don't start without a keyword strategy** — every video should target a specific search query
7. **Don't ignore the first 5 seconds** — hook or lose them
8. **Don't put the CTA at the end only** — front-load it (point 1)
9. **Don't skip B-roll** — talking head only = lower retention = lower ranking
10. **Don't compare yourself to channels with 1M subscribers** — compare to local competitors (there aren't many)
