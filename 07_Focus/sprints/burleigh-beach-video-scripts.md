*(Hold up one finger)*

One. This headland. You can walk here every single morning before work. It's not a tourist thing — the locals actually do it. Every day.

*(Start walking along the headland path, camera following)*

Two. The village. Burleigh Village isn't a shopping centre pretending to be a community. It's actual independent shops, actual regulars. You bump into the same people at the same coffee shop.

*(Stop, turn to camera with the coastline behind you)*

Three. The separation. You're on the Gold Coast, but Burleigh doesn't feel like the Gold Coast. It feels like its own town. People move here and they stop looking.

*(Beat, direct to camera)*

None of that shows up in a price guide. But it's the reason the median sits at two point two million and no one's in a hurry to sell.

I track every listing and every sale at fieldsestate.com.au."

- Walk the path for point two
- Stop at a spot where the coastline stretches north behind you for point three and the close

## Filming Notes for Will

**General tips:**
- Film in the morning light (golden hour or just after — Burleigh faces east so you'll get good light early)
- Selfie mode, phone at chest/chin height, not above your head
- Walk slowly — movement keeps it interesting but too fast looks shaky
- Talk like you're explaining this to a mate at the pub, not presenting
- One take per script is fine — imperfect feels more authentic on Facebook
- If you stumble, just pause and keep going — you can trim in edit

**Order to film:**
1. Video 3 first (on the beach/esplanade, easiest setup)
2. Video 1 second (beach to esplanade walk)
3. Video 2 last (headland — best light will be when the sun is a bit higher)

**Important:** Video 3 references homes below comparable values without naming specific addresses or giving a single valuation figure — this keeps it compliant with the editorial rules. If anyone asks in comments which properties, point them to the site.
