# Burleigh Beach Scripts - Product Review

## Session Status

This review is partially degraded. The workspace is missing the full metrics and page-map bundle, so the recommendations below focus on CTA clarity, destination match, and trust rather than claimed traffic performance.

## Product Verdict

The current scripts have a good top-of-funnel feel, but they all make the same product mistake: they end by sending the viewer back to the site broadly instead of giving them one matched next step.

For Sprint 1, that is the wrong trade. The sprint is trying to prove one measurable conversion path. These videos should not be treated as brand traffic. They should be treated as routed proof traffic.

## What To Change

1. Use one destination per video.
Video 1: Burleigh browse/proof surface only.
Video 2: Burleigh suburb-guide surface.
Video 3: Exact proof page or Burleigh pricing-gap surface.

2. Keep the spoken CTA short.
Good spoken CTA: "Search Burleigh Waters on Fields and compare the listings yourself."
Bad spoken CTA: "I break down every listing at fieldsestate.com.au."

3. Put the exact URL in the caption and pinned comment.
The spoken line should stay conversational. The exact destination belongs in text.

4. Do not ask the viewer to trust an uncheckable claim.
If Will says a home looks `$300,000+` below comparable evidence, the viewer needs an obvious proof path immediately after the video.

## Best Posting Order

1. Video 2 first.
Why: best location-message fit, most natural hook for filming at Burleigh Beach, least dependent on a specific proof page.

2. Video 3 second.
Why: strongest intent hook, but only if the destination is specific enough to verify the claim.

3. Video 1 last or rewrite harder.
Why: the core insight is solid, but the current close is too generic and the promise overlaps with Video 2.

## Revised Scripts

### Video 1

**Current issue:** Good lifestyle framing, weak destination. It tells viewers what Burleigh buys, then sends them to the whole website.

**Recommended destination:** Burleigh Waters browse page or Burleigh-specific proof page.

**Revised close:**

> That's not me saying it's cheap. It's not. But if you want to see the Burleigh Waters listings I'm tracking and compare them properly, go to Fields, search Burleigh Waters, and start with the homes closest to the beach.

**Caption CTA:**

> See the Burleigh Waters listings and valuation notes on Fields. Start with Burleigh Waters, not the homepage.

### Video 2

**Current issue:** This is the best script, but the CTA still falls back to generic browsing.

**Recommended destination:** Burleigh Waters suburb guide or "Is Now a Good Time to Buy in Burleigh Waters?" surface.

**Revised close:**

> None of that shows up in a price guide. But it does explain why Burleigh Waters stays expensive and tightly held. If you want the data behind it, start with our Burleigh Waters guide on Fields.

**Caption CTA:**

> Start with the Burleigh Waters guide on Fields, then compare the listings and recent sales yourself.

### Video 3

**Current issue:** Strongest proof hook, highest trust risk. "The Most Underpriced Street" is too absolute unless the destination proves it instantly.

**Recommended destination:** Exact listing analysis page. If that is not ready, use a Burleigh pricing-gap page or Burleigh-filtered results page.

**Title change:**

> One of the biggest pricing gaps we're tracking in Burleigh Waters right now

**Revised middle and close:**

> There are homes listed roughly eight hundred metres from this beach where the asking price is sitting hundreds of thousands below the comparable-sales evidence we're seeing nearby.
>
> That does not automatically mean "bargain." It could be condition, seller urgency, layout, or something the photos are not showing properly.
>
> But when the gap is that big, it is worth checking the evidence before everyone else does.
>
> I've put the Burleigh page in the comments so you can compare the sales and judge it for yourself.

**Caption CTA:**

> One of the biggest pricing gaps we're tracking in Burleigh Waters right now. Compare the evidence yourself on Fields.

## Final Product Call

If Will only films one tomorrow, it should be Video 2.

If Will films two, do Video 2 and Video 3.

If the team cannot point Video 3 to a verifiable destination before posting, do not lead with the strongest pricing claim. Fields wins on transparent methodology, not mystery.
