# Fields Voice Guide — How Content Should Sound

> **Purpose:** Every piece of content — Facebook posts, video transcripts, articles, ad copy — must pass this guide. Agents and AI systems use this as the quality standard.
> **Rule:** If content is compliant but boring, it fails. If it's engaging but non-compliant, it fails. It must be BOTH.

---

## Will's Voice

Will sounds like a smart friend who works in property data and genuinely finds it interesting. He's not an agent trying to sell you something. He's not a professor lecturing. He's a guy who looked at the numbers and wants to show you what he found because it's actually surprising.

**Tone:** Direct, curious, slightly provocative. Like a mate who says "you won't believe what I found" over a beer.

**Energy:** Confident but honest. Will states what the data shows and isn't afraid to point out when something doesn't add up. But he never pretends to know more than he does.

**Personality markers:**
- Uses "I" — "I pulled the data", "I found something interesting"
- Speaks TO the reader — "you", "your suburb", "your budget"
- Admits uncertainty — "could mean a lot of things", "there's always a reason"
- Creates tension — presents a contradiction or surprise, then explains it
- Never sells — no "call us today", no "don't miss out", no urgency language

## The Quality Scale (1-10)

### 10/10 — Stop-scrolling, comment-driving, share-worthy
```
800 metres from Burleigh Beach.

Some current listings are sitting $300,000+ below what comparable
sales in the same streets have actually transacted for.

That gap could mean a lot of things — condition, motivation, strategy.
But it's the kind of data point worth knowing about before someone
else does.
```
**Why it works:** Leads with a specific, surprising claim. Creates urgency without advice. Makes you want to know which properties.

### 8/10 — Strong hook, engages, drives clicks
```
Would you pay $1,800,000 for a 3-bed in Robina?

On paper, comparable 3-bed homes in this pocket have sold for
$1.3M to $1.5M.

But this one sits on 840sqm with DA approval to subdivide.

So you're not really paying for a 3-bed house. You're paying for
the land play.

Is the premium worth it — or are you better off buying the house
next door for $500K less?

Tell me 👇
```
**Why it works:** Direct question, specific numbers, reframes the obvious, invites opinion.

### 6/10 — Decent information, weak engagement
```
Robina has 52 active listings. The median asking price is about
$1.35 million. At that level, the common pattern is a 3 to 4
bedroom house.

Data source: fieldsestate.com.au
```
**Why it fails:** No hook. No tension. No reason to stop scrolling. It's a data card, not a conversation. Nobody comments on this.

### 4/10 — Compliant but dead
```
Fields is tracking 35 active listings in Burleigh Waters right now,
against 267 sold records in the database. The median asking price
being quoted in the suburb is about $2.2 million. The combination
worth watching is the price level and the size of the active pool
at the same time.
```
**Why it fails:** Passive voice. No hook. "The combination worth watching" — watching for whom? Why? This reads like a database query formatted as a paragraph.

### 2/10 — Robot wrote this
```
In the current Fields snapshot, the suburb is sitting at about a
$2.2 million median asking price with 35 active listings in the
database. This does not tell you what will happen next. It does
tell you the suburb is being traded in a relatively tight live pool.
```
**Why it fails:** "The suburb is sitting at" — nobody talks like this. "Traded in a relatively tight live pool" — this is financial jargon, not human language.

## Good vs Bad — Side by Side

### Property Story

**BAD (4/10):**
```
Same suburb does not mean same pricing logic. 27 Seville Circuit,
Burleigh Waters is a renovated 4 bedroom home with a pool asking
$1,699,000. That is the kind of listing that reminds you how much
location within the suburb still matters.
```

**GOOD (8/10):**
```
This Burleigh Waters home is the reason suburb medians lie.

Renovated 4-bed. Pool. Asking $1,699,000.

On features alone, buyers will compare it with homes that sold
for $1.95M to $2.1M.

So what's the catch? The back fence tells the story.

Full analysis 👇
```
**Difference:** The good version leads with a provocative claim ("medians lie"), creates curiosity ("the back fence tells the story"), and gives you a reason to click.

### Data Video Transcript

**BAD (4/10):**
```
Everyone quotes the median price. Hardly anyone shows what it
usually looks like. Right now, the asking-price midpoint on the
priced Robina listings we are tracking is about one point three
five million dollars.
```

**GOOD (8/10):**
```
Everyone talks about median prices. Nobody shows you what it
actually looks like. Right now, $1.35 million in Robina gets you
a 3-to-4-bed house, probably 2 baths, built around 2005 to 2015.
But here's the thing — more than half these listings don't even
show a price. So what are buyers actually paying?
```
**Difference:** The good version gets to the tangible picture faster, then creates a question the viewer wants answered.

### Question/Opinion Post

**BAD (5/10):**
```
Would you pay more than $1,800,000 for a 3 bedroom, 2 bathroom
house in Robina? 83 Glen Eagles Drive is currently being marketed
above that level. Listings at this level usually get judged on
more than the bedroom count alone.
```

**GOOD (9/10):**
```
Would you pay $1.8M for a 3-bed in Robina?

Comparable homes: $1.3M to $1.5M.
This one: 840sqm with DA approval to subdivide.

So is it a house — or a land play?

Tell me 👇
```
**Difference:** Short. Punchy. The reframe ("house or land play?") gives people something to argue about.

## Content Type Templates

### Data Video (40-60 seconds)
```
[HOOK — surprising number or contradiction, first 3 seconds]
[CONTEXT — what this means, make it tangible]
[THE TWIST — the thing most people miss or get wrong]
[SOFT CTA — "link in the comments" or "see the full breakdown"]
```
Must feel like Will talking to a friend, not presenting to a boardroom.

### Question/Opinion Post
```
[DIRECT QUESTION — would you? which one? guess the price?]
[JUST ENOUGH CONTEXT — 2-3 lines max, specific numbers]
[THE REFRAME — change how they think about it]
[INVITATION — "tell me 👇" or "which one and why?"]
```
Must provoke a response. If nobody would comment, it's not a question post.

### Property Story
```
[HOOK — contradiction, surprise, or "this one will surprise you"]
[THE SETUP — property details, brief]
[THE CATCH — what makes this interesting (price gap, re-listing, location trade-off)]
[CTA — exact property page link]
```
Must tell a STORY, not describe a listing.

### Article Promo
```
[TEASER STAT — one striking number from the article]
[WHY IT MATTERS — one sentence connecting it to the reader]
[LINK — direct to article]
```
Never "we wrote an article about X." Always lead with the most interesting finding.

## Rules Applied Together

1. **Compliant:** No advice, no predictions, no single valuation in headlines, value framing, exact figures with sources
2. **Engaging:** Hook in first line, tension or contradiction, specific numbers, personal voice, invites response
3. **Concise:** If it can be said in fewer words, say it in fewer words. Facebook rewards short punchy copy.
4. **Visual:** Describe what things look like, not just what the numbers say. "3-bed, built around 2005, no pool" tells a story. "$1.35M median" does not.
5. **Provocative but honest:** Create tension with data. "This home is listed $300K below comparables" is provocative AND factual.

---

## The Test

Before publishing any content, ask:
1. Would I stop scrolling for this? (If no → rewrite the hook)
2. Would I comment on this? (If no → add a question or reframe)
3. Does this sound like Will talking? (If no → remove jargon, add "I" and "you")
4. Is there a specific, surprising number in the first 2 lines? (If no → find one)
5. Would a seller read this and feel respected? (If no → reframe the trade-off)
